Altteld's Record Condemned!
TO THE CITIZENS OF BALTIMORE:
Four years ago, I thought it my duty to assist in
defeating Mr. E. Milton Altfeld, then, as now, can-
didate for the State Senate. My reasons were these:
1. Altfeld, as a member of the Legislature, on
April 6th, 1914, voted for a most vicious
segregation bill. (See House Journal 1914,
Vol. 2, Page 3650).
2. Altfeld opposed colored men aspiring to
public office, and, in his campaign, told a
white audience: "These Negroes should be
kept in their places." (See Afro-American,
October 30th, 1926.
For this hostility to our race, I urge all colored
voters, regardless of party, to defeat Altfeld, by
voting for his opponent, Paul Berman.
WARNER T. MCGUINN.
Berman's Record Endorsed!
1425 McCulloh Street,
Baltimore, Md., Oct. 7, 1930.
Mr. Paul Berman,
217 Court House, City.
My dear Mr. Berman: I want to let you know
how very highly I appreciate your recent visit with
me, and others, to the two schools in Virginia for
delinquent colored boys and girls. Visible evidence
of your enyojment of every phase of the trip were
manifest on every hand.
I sincerely trust that you may be elected a mem-
ber of the State Senate, not merely because you are,
in every sense, capable and competent, (and I have
known you from your youth) but because I know.
that the members of our group will ever find a sin-
cere and devoted friend in you. And, especially,
because I am persuaded that you will exert every
effort towards improving conditions for the care of
colored delinquents.
Faithfully yours,
GEORGE F. BRAGG, JR.

Your Duty to your RACE and to your self respect
require the defeat of Altfeld by the Election of
