Dedicate Cheltenham Gymnasium in Oct.

BALTIMORE
Dedication of the new $80,-000 gymnasium at the Cheltenham School for Boys, originally scheduled for Sunday afternoon, has been postponed to one day early next month, it was learned on Thursday.

The new brick building is one-story high, with a basement equipped with showers and wash rooms. The main floor is suitable for indoor games and may also be used as an auditorium.

Swimming Pool Held Up
Addition of a swimming pool to the building is being held up pending granting of additional funds by the next legislature, it was reported.

While the dedication date has tentatively been set for October 12, it is expected that a definite date will be set at a meeting of the board of managers on next Thursday.

N.C. Holds Guards after Convicts Flee

GOLDSBORO, N.C.—Following the escape of two long-term convicts, James Morgan and Richard Brooks, both 35, from the State Hospital for the insane, last week, J. F. Green and Elmore Morrissey, both white watchmen at the hospital, are being held pending an investigation.

Morgan and Brooks, sentenced for robbery, sawed through bars in a second-story cell block and climbed down a rope made of sheets. The two, recaptured five miles south of this city, had made an attempt to escape three weeks previously.
