Noted for Fair Dealings
Judge Ulman was noted for
being as fair in his dealings with
his fellow citizens in his non-
professional life as he tried to be
on the bench.
During his term as president
of the Urban League board, he
led a delegation to protest a
lynching on the Eastern Shore to
Governor Albert Ritchie in spite
of the fact that the Governor
had appointed him.
When the Van Nuys Antilynch-
ing Bill was before the United
States Senate in 1936, Judge Ul-
man made a private appeal to the
Senate for its passing.
He also lodged a protest with,
the Postal Telegraph Company in
1941 when the company failed to
assign to duty six colored youths
who had been hired for service.
