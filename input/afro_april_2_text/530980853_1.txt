HELD FOR COURT ON
NUMBERS CHARGE
Charged with accepting money as
bets on numbers, William Henry, 41,
was ordered held for action of the
Criminal Court after a hearing in
the Northwestern police court on
Monday.
According to the policeman, who
made the arrest, Henry was sitting
on a bootblack stand in a shoe re-
pair shop in the 1400 block Gilmor
Street, when the money was given
to him.
The patrolman alleged that Hen-
ry accepted the money and gave
the bettor a slip as receipt. A book.
said by the policeman to have been
used by Henry, was shown at the
hearing, before Magistrate Pohl-
haus.
The alleged number writing is
said to have occurred on April 15.
Bail was set at $500.

Arraigned in the Juvenile Court
on charges preferred by policemen,

ted to the Cheltenham Reforma-
tory, this week.
The committments were for pe-
riods ranging from one year to four
years. The boys who were sent to
the school were Gordon Giles, 15,
1119 McCulloh Street, Marshall
Dorsey, 11, 927 Stricker Street; Ir-
vin Gordon, 14, 707 Edmondson
Avenue, and Robert Garrett, 14, of
1155 Carrollton Avenue. The boys'
records show that they were con-
victed of having committed felon-
ies. Two of the boys were second
cffenders and at large on parole.
