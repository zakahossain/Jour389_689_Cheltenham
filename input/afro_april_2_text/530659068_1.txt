Virtual slavery, peonage
and a chain gang exist at
the House of Reformation
for Colored Boys at Chel-
tenham, Prince Georges
County, Md., Dr. Ernest
Lyon told the three local
ministers' conferences at
their various meetings this
week.
Dr. Lyon said he paid a visit to
the institution without notice and
there he found 300 boys from Balti-
more city and the state of Maryland
ragged. and barerooted despite the
cold weather, hair uncombed and
the older boys hired out in "this
Maryland free state". to work on
plantations surrounding.
Like a
chain gang, he said, they stay out
all week and come back home Sat-
uraday for a bath.
Despite the dirty and laborious
work these boys have to do, bath-
ing facilities are provided only once
a week.
"The boys are there from the ages
of 10 to 21. I went into a room where
there were 75 little boys. The teach-
er. poorly educated himself, tried to
have one of the youngsters stand up
and read. But they couldn't. They
have had no educational advantages
whatever.
"The conditions at the institution
are shameful. If I weren't a min-
ister I would tell what kind of a
shame it is."
Rev. Bragg Speaks
The Rev. Dr. George F. Bragg, for
over 25 years a member of the Chel-
tenham board, declared that the
board at present consisted of 12 per-
sons, two members appointed by the
governor, two by the mayor and the
remaining eight elected by the board
itself. The institution is private and
is owned by the board of trustees.
Most of the members, he said, reside
in Prince Georges County and priv-
ate interests and politics have pre-
vented any change in the personnel.
Dr. Lyon referred to the buildings
as old and falling apart. The super-
intendent. Mr. Pyles, he said. has
only one leg. when the institution
should have men with at "least four
legs" to keep up with the children
committed there to be reformed.
The A. M. E. ministers named the
following committee to cooperate
with ministers' meetings of other de-
nominations in asking the governor
and mayor to withhold appropria-
tions from Cheltenham or replace
the present personnel with the prop-
