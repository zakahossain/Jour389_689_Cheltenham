Charles Mantley 18 years old of 709.
Mulberry St., Charles Bundy 18 and
Robert Young were arranged in part
1 of the crimnal court on last Monday
for the larceny of two cases of whis-
key from the Seegle Wholesale Liquor
House on Lombard St. near Front St.,
on September 13 and the attempt to
repeat the act on September 20. Bun
dy had been parolea for the same
offense last May, but had violated the
parole.
The boys claimed in court that
they had stolen the whiskey and soid
it to Joseph Alexander, who keeps a
pool parlor on the corner of Saratoga
and Pine Sts. Alexander was arrest-
ed for receiving stolen goods but was
declared by the court to be not guilty.
Bundy was committed to the House of
Correction at Cheltenham: Charles
Mantley was given six months in jail
to take effect from September 20 last;
Robinson Young was declared, not
guilty by the court.
ST. MICHAELS' HOME
DESTROYED BY FIRE
St. Michaels, Md., Dec. 11.-Sun-
day being a fine day quite a large
crowd was out to hear our pastor.
Collection was $31.25. Mrs. Cathar-
ine Mitchell who spent Thanksgiving
in Baltimore visiting relatives and
friends has returned home. Miss
Brooks, of Williamsburg, was the
guest of Mr. and Mrs. Coulbourne on
Sunday. Mr. Henry Brook's home
was destroyed by fire. Mr. John Miles
and his little son Washington, are
visiting in Philadelphia. Mr. and
Mrs. F. S. Jewett and daughter Ber-
nice, spent a few days in Baltimore.
