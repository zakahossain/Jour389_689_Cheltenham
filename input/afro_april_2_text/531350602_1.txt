Gov. Allows
3 Million for
Institutions
BALTIMORE
Seven State institutions for
colored people and nine State-
aided institutions will receive in
excess of $3,000,000 during the
next two years as a result of
the State Legislature's recent ap-
proval of Governor Herbert R.
O'Conor's budget recommenda-
tions, an AFRO survey shows
this week.
In addition, $350,000 of this
amount will go toward equaliza-
tion of teachers' salaries in the
Maryland counties and $50,000
will provide scholarships for
Maryland students who desire to
study at institutions of higher
learning outside the State.
A comparison of the O'Conor
appropriations with those during
the years 1938-39, when the late
Harry W. Nice was Governor,
shows that Governor O'Conor has
allotted twice as much to our in-
stitutions as did his predecessor.
Mr. Nice appropriated only
$1,392,204 as compared with the
present governor's three million.

Largest increases over the Nice
administration provided by Gov-
ernor O'Conor are for the Chel-
tenham School for Boys (which
during the Nice administration
was in process of becoming a
State institution), which during
Mr. Nice's term received only
$90,000 for that two-year period
as compared with the $558,378
provided by Mr. O'Conor for the
next two years.
Morgan State College (which
was not State institution during
the Nice regime) will receive a
total of $519,800 during the next
two years as compared with the
$120,000 it received during the
previous administration.
Princess Anne College, which
was denied appropriations for
new buildings during the Nice
term, which allowed only $51,966,
will be able to add at least one
new building with the $151,966
allowed by the present governor.
The Maryland School for Girls
at Glenburnie will receive twice
as much ($142,290) during the
next two years as it received
under Mr. Nice, when it was
allowed $77,280.
