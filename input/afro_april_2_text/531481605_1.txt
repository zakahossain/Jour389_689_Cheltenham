However, he does not feel that

"Some of the boys now serving
in the armed forces were errants
in that era; today it is their young-
er brothers who are under court
supervision," Mr. King stated.
"Since war has been declared,
boys seem to sense the uncertain-
ty of life. They have more
money to spend and parents have
less time to devote to them," he
said.
Devoted to the interest and wel-
fare of youths, Mr. King feels that
delinquency would be greatly re-
duced if there was an enlarged
institution for the feeble-minded,
a training school for boys to re-
place Cheltenham, and a study
home for children's mentality.
Most Delinquents Ignorant
"Most delinquents are ignorant
or come from low-mentality fami-
lies," he pointed out.
Mr. King's duties include in-
vestigating children's cases; in-
specting their homes; checking
tehir mental background and their
school records; and supervising
those placed on probation.
A former local teacher and pos-
tal employee, Mr. King is a grad-
uate of Coppin Teachers' College
and attended Morgan State Col-
lege and Columbia University
!(NYC.).
He is a member of Omega Psi
Phi Fraternity and St. James PE
Church.
For four years he was
president of the Social Workers'
Round Table.
