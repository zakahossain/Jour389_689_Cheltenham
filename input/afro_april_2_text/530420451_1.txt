Rev. Geo. F. Bragg and Mr.
Bernard Taylor, members of the
Board of Directors of the House
of Reformation in Prince Georges
County. Maryland, paid a visit to
the institution last Tuesday.
They were received by Superin-
tendent Pyles, served dinner, and
spent a large part of the after-
noon in inspecting grounds and
buildings and the work of the
353 boys who are inmates there.
The committee of the February
Grand Jury had already given
Cheltenham a clean bill so that
the members of the Board were
not surprised to find both school
work and shop work in full blast
and an apparently satisfied set of
youths busily occupied.
On this last point, Superintend-
ent Pyles informed the visiting
Board members that despite the
fact, that there are no walls around
the institution, there are very few
boys who attempt to escape,
He
said that freuently a whole gang
is hired out to neighboring farm-
ers. and sent away in auto trucks
under the charge of some older
boys. At night fall however, they
invaribly showed up again, and
none was missing.
Rev. Bragg is one of the older
members of Cheltenham Board
but Mr. Taylor was making his
first visit to the institution since
his appointment last year.
