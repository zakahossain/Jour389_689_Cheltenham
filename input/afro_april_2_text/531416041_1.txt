SOLDIERS AND SAILORS
ALL NOT GOOD
"Dear Mr. Freeman, in answer
to the soldiers who want to know
why people treat them mean. In
the first place they bring it all
on themselves. I will tell about
my dealing with them. I
saw
about eight boys one Saturday
night in a spot on Tremont Street,
almost attack a woman. It was
about the worst thing I had ever
seen. I tried to get them to leave
the woman alone but was told to
get out of the way.
"I saw the boys one night pull
a knife on a doorman because he
told them to sit down. I. myself,
had my coat torn. People stop
ped coming to this particular spot
because of them. I spoke to a
sergeant about them and he told
me there was nothing he could
do, they were out to have a good
time.
"I have seen the boys go in a
place, sit at a table and drink an-
other party's drinks. I was in the
National Guard and if any of the
boys acted like I see them act, I
know what they would get when
we had a camp meeting. These
boys think that because they are
in the army and do not want to
be there, they should do as they
please.
"I find that the Southern boys
are the most ignorant to deal
with. The first thing they do is
pull a knife on you. All that I
have said in answer to the soldiers
is what I have seen in a place
where I worked. The people are
with the boys 100 per cent but
they make it tough for them-
selves."
