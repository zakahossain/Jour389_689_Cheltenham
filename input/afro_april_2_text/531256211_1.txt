ENGLEWOOD, N.J.
The State corrective school
for boys at Cheltenham,
Md., is a place where 450
"Dead End Kids" live under
conditions that are many
times worse than conditions
portrayed in the movie of
that name, according to
Raymond Hobson, until re-
cently an instructor there.
Hobson says that he was dis-
missed from his post the first of
January, because:
He spoke to Superintendent
Benjamin Sunderland, white,
without addressing him by
name;
He has constantly refused to
humble himself before officials
and the 60 or 70 white instruc-
tors there;
He was too popular with the
boys imprisoned there.
"Rotten Set-up"
"I spoke up when things
seemed wrong to me," he said
Thursday. "They told me to mind
my own business and they would
mind theirs.
"It is a rotten set-up," he con-
tinued. "The boys are supposed
to be prepared to re-enter society
with a new point of view. Actual-
ly they are prepared for nothing.
They are encouraged to lie and
cheat. No control is exercised over
them. They are cruelly beaten.
"One boy was beaten and con-
fined to the hospital nine months.
He was struck in the face with a
bunch of keys, but the official
report was that he was hurt play-
ing football. On the road to re-
covery he was beaten again with
the result that both of his eyes
were closed. His nose bled for four
days.
No Report Made
There was no report on his
condition from the hospital. A
cottage master struck the boy
whose name is Fred Ames. The
superintendent knows these con-
ditions but says nothing."
