Sets Fire to Stable;
War Vet Held Insane
Jeckariah Anderson Fires Building
to See Apparatus Arrive.
Arrested on charges of arson when
seen setting fire to the American
Transfer stables at West and Peach
Streets, Zeckariah Anderson, 39,
world war veteran, 141 W. West
Street, was judged insane by police
physicians and committed to the
Municipal Hospital after a hearing
in the Southern police station, Fri-
day.
Anderson, testimony showed, had
a mania for seeing and hearing fire
apparatus. He was seen setting fire
to the stables, which did very little
damage, being suppressed by several
witnesses. Relatives of the accused
man stated that he has been dement-
ed since injured during the World
War.
Hold Man Here on
Bogus Check Charge
Said by poilce to have been des-
James A. Whitehurst, 601 W. North
Avenue, by means of a bogus check
drawn on the Union Trust Company
on December 24, Samuel Turner, 26,
1529 McCulloh Street, was held for
grand jury action after a hearing in
the Northwestern police station,
Tuesday.
Turner admitted tendering the
check because of lack of money for
Christmas and being unemployed.
