Colored golfers agreed with rep-
resentatives of the city to play
on the three municipal courses
until after $20,000 improvements
are made on the Carroll Park
course which has been set aside
for them. This agreement follows
Maryland Court of Appeals' re-
versal of lower court decision
opening all city-owned courses to
colored golfers.
The Federal Public Housing
Authority has indicated its will
ingness to build a permanent sub-
urban housing project rather than
temporary homes for war work
ers.
Since eight million dollars was
appropriated two months ago for
2,000 such units, the Baltimore
Housing Authority has not yet
, agreed on a site for the location
of 1,600 of them.

Three Die in Fires
Three persons, Mrs. Elizabeth
Cooper, 37; Mrs. Beulah Robin-
son, 28. and William Anderson,
43, died as the result of week-
end fires.
The two women were fatally
burned in a blaze at the Cat's
Paw Rubber Heel Factory. An-
derson sustained a fractured
skull by jumping from a third-
floor window when fire began
in his home.
Five men who won places on
the eligible list for appointment
to the police department are
Leon King, Clifton H. James,
Clarence J. Stevens, Queener E.
Willis and Lloyd G. Johnson.
Walter C. Jones, teacher for
five years at the Cheltenham
School for Boys, resigned in pro-
test against the administration
of Vance E. Thomas, white, super-
intendent, whom he blamed for
racial tension at the school.
