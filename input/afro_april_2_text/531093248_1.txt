Oliver Quits Job
at Cheltenham
BALTIMORE-
Claiming dissatisfaction with
conditions at the Cheltenham
School of Reformation, Basil B.
Oliver, physical educational di-
rector, resigned from the teaching
staff of the school, Wednesday.
Oliver, a native of Burkesville,
Va., and a former football star
at Virginia State University, stated
that he planned to accept the post
of educational director at the Din-
widdie, Va., CCC Camp.
The former All-CIAA tackle
was appointed to Cheltenham on
January 15, 1935. Other than
stating that he was dissatisfied
with conditions, Mr. Oliver de-
clined to elaborate on his resig-
nation. He graduated from Va.
State in 1933.

Man Is Freed in
Torch Murder
BALTIMORE-
Charged with first degree mur-
der in the torch death of his al-
lege sweetheart, Miss Ethel Rich-
ardson, George Scott was found
not guilty by Judge Duke Bond in
the criminal court, Wednesday.
Officers in their testimony said
that the man poured a gallon of
gasoline on the young woman,
with whom he lived at 1423 May
Street, on January 19. Defense ob-
jections to the woman's alleged
death-bed statement which were
sustained, aided in Scott's being
freed.
The other five persons whose
permits were revoked are: James
Watkins of 811 N. Carey Street;
Frank W. Mills of Hebron, Md.;
Charles H. Smith of Delmar, Md.;
Delmar. Md.
George Banks of 1155 N. Carey
Street and Edward Edwards of 411
