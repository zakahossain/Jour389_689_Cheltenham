BALTIMORE
| Joseph C. Briscoe, principal of
Carver Vocational School, has a
full-time job trying to gear his

JOSEPH C. BRISCOE
school's curriculum to meet the
war-time interests and needs of
his pupils.
Fully 55 per cent of his student
body is engaged in part-time work
after school hours.
Working Hours Vary
In most cases it's an individual
matter. Some go to their jobs
immediately after school and
work until 1 and 2 a.m. Still oth-
ers start work at 2 a.m. and quit
at 7 in the morning.
Health and recreational prob-
lems must be considered, Mr. Bris-
coe pointed out, and the task of
revamping the school schedule to
benefit all concerned is a tremen-
dous one.
"We have litle time for extra-
curricula activities now," the prin-
cipal asserted. "Most of our boys
and girls feel a serious obligation
to their work which precludes ev-
en such activities as a victory
corps."
Mr. Briscoe, with a refined de
meanor, fights for what he believes
is right.
On Cheltenham Board
A member of the board of the
Cheltenham School for Boys eight
years, he has been diligent in ex
(ercising his powers, and has main
tained that segregation at the
school be abolished, unequivo
cably.
When affairs at Provident Hos
pital, of which he has been a
board member for many years,
were not being managed to meet
his standards, he refused to go
along "for the sake of peace" and
sent in his resignation.
In adidtion the Vocational prin
cipal is an active board member
of the Druid Hill YMCA, is on
the Boy Scout district committee,
and still finds time to continue
ſteaching Sunday school at St.
Katherine PE Church after thirty
years' service.
Mr. Briscoe is married to the
former Mrs. Virginia Hall. a di
rector in the Department of Pub-
lic Welfare. They live at 1301
Madison Avenue.
More Fowl, Seafood
The war hasn't seriously affect-
ed his personal life to any extent
although Mr. Briscoe admits that
he is eating more fowl and sea.
food than formerly.
He'll still be able to see his
favorite teams play baseball, a
sport about which he is quite
enthusiastic.
