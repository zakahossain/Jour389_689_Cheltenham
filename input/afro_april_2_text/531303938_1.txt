Ask State for
$20,000 More
for Cheltenham
BALTIMORE
A committee interested in the
Cheltenham School for Boys ap-
peared before the ways and
means committee of the Mary-
land Legislature on Tuesday and
requested that a supplemental
budget of $20,700 be approved
for the institution.
A total of $11,700 of the
'amount requested was for operat-
ing expenses, including $7,600 for
food supplies and $4,100 for
wearing apparel.
The remaining $9,020 re-
quested was for salaries and
wages, including a $300 increase
| in the salaries of the two colored
parole officers and other em-
ployees.
Governor Asked $500.000
In his budget submitted to the
legislature in January, Governor
Herbert R. O'Conor requested
over a half-million dollars for
the institution, including $20,000
for additional salaries; $5,000 for
operating expenses; and $225,000
for new buildings.
This amount, however, did not
include sufficient funds to allow
purchase of more adequate food
supplies and kitchen equipment.
wearing apparel or increase in
salaries for some employees, in-
cluding the parole officers.
Those who made the request
for a supplmenetal budget in-
cluded Edward S. Lewis, a mem-
ber of the board of managers of
the school; Joseph H. Blandford,
president of the board; Colonel
H. C. Jones, State employment
commission; J. Abner Hall and
Sidney Charles.
