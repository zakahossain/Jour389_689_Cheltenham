BALTIMORE -The local
NAACP fired the opening
gun on Sunday in its cam-
paign to end bans against
colored patrons in certain
Baltimore stores by calling
the discrimination officially
to the attention of the Re-
tail Merchants' Association.
The drive was launched at the
association's mass meeting at
; Sharp Street ME Church, and will
continue with increasing vigor,
Mrs. Lillie M. Jackson, president
of the NAACP unit, said.
Governor Harry W. Nice was
commended in a telegram sent
him by the branch during the
NAACP meeting.
The governor was praised for
his stand on the equalization of
salaries for all teachers in Mary-
land and also for his interest in
Cheltenham School for Boys by
seeking the resignation of the
present board. He was further
asked to make two justices of the
peace, George W. McMechen and
William I. Gosnell, active.
