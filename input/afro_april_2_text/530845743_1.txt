The G.O.P. Platform
The Maryland Republican State Convention
included two admirable planks and one in-
definite one in its platform as finally adopted.
If victorious, the Maryland G.O.P. pledges
self to erect an asylum for feeble-minded
colored children. Funds for such an asylum
were authorized several years ago by the
Democratic legislature but Governor Ritchie
vetoed the appropriation.
Republicans also pledge much needed reforms
at the Cheltenham and Melvale reform schools,
where incompetence and immorality have been
long uncovered without Democrats or Republi-
cans becoming perturbed about these matters
heretofore.
In these two planks the Republican party
speaks openly and directly and no one need mis-
understand their meaning.
The third plank which favors the "general
improvement of school facilities for cclored
children throughout the state" is neither direct
nor explicit.
"Facilities" is a rather general term. Under
such a plank, the school bulidings, or the
grounds, may be improved and the number of
teachers increased, and new types of schools
added.
Specifically, Maryland is educationally de-
ficient in providing college and professional
training for Negroes such as it provides out
of tax funds for whites at the State University
of Maryland.
Secondly, Maryland illegally fixes by law the
minimum pay for white county teachers at $600
to $1,200 a year and for colored county teachers
at $40 to $120 a month.
In view of the fact that Maryland Democrats
have no Negro plank, the G.O.P. platform rep-
represents marked advancement.
However, if it means to equalize county
teachers' salaries and give Negro boys and girls
a chance to attend a state college or medical
school, it is unfortunate that explicit language
could not have been used.
