$2,686 Estate
Given to Heirs

BALTIMORE
The $2,686 estate of Mrs.
Charity McKee, widow of the
late president of Florida A. and
M. College was distributed to her
heirs here, this week.

Beneficiaries included:

Mrs. Philly Mason of St. Louis,
$1,283; Mrs. Eudora Tucker,
$1283.

Boys’ Home Gets $150

Home for Friendless Boys, $150,
by way the Virgin Council
No. 42, Daughters of Elks; $150
from the Neighbors in Training
Club; Miss Alice Price, $25.12;
Bishop Norman Bishop and Ruth
Bishop, $100; Miss Elizabeth
Smith, $100; and the Rev. Shel-
ton Bishop, Hutchins C. Bishop,
Vivian Bishop, and Andrew
Bishop, $10.62 each.

Mrs. Augusta Boothe, a niece,
was omitted from the list of ben-
eficiaries because the widow
said she had made a settlement
on her before her death.

Jones Fired by
Recreation Dept.

BALTIMORE
J. Alvin Jones, Jr., supervisor
in the recreational department of
Baltimore, was recently dis-
missed $22 in charges of disorderly
conduct and assaulting an officer,
was dismissed from his job on
Saturday.

At a meeting of the seven-man
board of the recreation depart-
ment, it was apparent that Mr.
Jones, former Lexington Market
clerk, was guilty of conduct un-
becoming a recreational leader
and city employee.

They had reference to the so-
licitations at a dance given for
the benefit of a Social Club
on last February 28.

At that time, Mr. Jones, who
lives at 1633 W. Baltimore
Street, was involved in a disturb-
ance in the Odd Fellows’ Hall,
Park and Lanvale Streets,
which subsequently resulted
by charges of Clara Butler and
other officers.

6 Vacancies Now
at Cheltenham

BALTIMORE
The recent death of Dr. Wil-
liam H. Debnam, trustee, of
Durham, brings to six the num-
ber of vacancies on the board of
managers of the Cheltenham
School for Boys.

The terms of Enoch Harlan,
Edward J. Hall, Dr. J. B. John-
son, Alpheus K. Williams and James
L. Scrimair, expired last June, but
Governor O’Conor has not named
anyone to fill the vacancies.

Trial of Shore
Suspects Shifted

BALTIMORE
Although it is expected that
the two men indicted for the
murder of Harvey Pritchard,
white, Stockton, Md., farmer, will
go to trial next week, the case
has not been entered on the dock-
et of the Baltimore County Cir-
cuit Court at Towson, where it
was moved last week, the Afro
learned on Friday morning
October.

The change of venue from
Worcester County on the Eastern
Shore was granted by a judge of
the First Judicial Circuit Court.
Charged with the murder of the
farmer are Arthur Collick, 28,
and Charles Absurd, 17, both of
Stockton.
