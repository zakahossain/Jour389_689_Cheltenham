The Monumental City
Weekly News Summary
Cut this out and send it to your soldier boy in your next letter.
BALTIMORE-Dr. Francis M.
Wood, 65, director of colored
schools since 1927, died Friday
night at Johns Hopkins Hospital.
Six munitions workers were
killed and fifty-two others injured
in an explosion at the Triumph
Explosives Company in Elkton,
Md.
The dead are: Mrs. Christine
Erby, Wilmington; Mrs. Shirley
Gilmor, Coatesville, Pa.; Melvin
Cole, Stillpond, Pa.; Willie Crad-
dock, Dover, Del.; Harry Rian,
Dover; and an unidentified man.
The Cheltenham School for
Boys was promised an all-colored
staff in a resolution passed by
the interracial board of managers.
Provident Medic Resigns
Dr. Sarah E. Lewis, house
physician at Provident Hospital
for six years, resigned.
The Maryland State Conference
of Social Welfare discussed hous-
ing and delinquency problems in
a two-day meet at the Lord Balti-
more Hotel.
The war housing project to be
built at Fairfield, Md., was ap
proved to contain 248 instead of
400 units.
Church News
The annual conference of the
AME Church was held at Trinity
AME Church, with Bishop M. H.
Davis presiding, assisted by Bish-
op David H. Sims of Philadel-
phia.
Theodore R. McKeldin, new
mayor of Baltimore, made his
first public address since his elec-
tion Tuesday, to this conference.
Social Activities
Mrs. Mary Church Terrell, lec-
turer and author, was guest speak-
er for Alpna Gamma Chapter of
Delta Sigma Theta Sorority at
Morgan State College, opening
May Week services.
Miss Kathleen Mumby, Morgan
senior, reigned as queen at May
Day exercises.
Dooley Wilson, singing star of
"Casablanca," was entertained by
Mr. and Mrs. Noah Thompson.
Mrs. Dorothy N. Washington,
21, widow of Carl M. Washington,
who was drowned in March while
on duty with the U.S. Coast Guard
at Fort McHenry, became the
bride of Earl Lyons, 21, another
Coast Guardsman at the same sta-
tion.
Entertainment Notes
The opera study group of the
Studio of Creative Arts appeared
at Grace Memorial Baptist Church.
The Association of Colored
Musicians presented Miss Carol
Brice, contralto, in recital at
Sharp Street Methodist Church.
A symphony orchestra, directed
by A. Jack Thomas, presented a
concert at Dunbar High School.
Sports Events
Cocoa Kid of New Haven, Conn.,
won a 15-round decision over
Johnny Brown of Baltimore.
