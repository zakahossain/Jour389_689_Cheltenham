MAYOR HAYES AND CHELTENHAM.
Largely through the efforts of
Councilman Watty, and Mr. Cum-
mings and Rev. Mr. Bragg, members
of the Board of Managers of the
House of Reformation for colored
boys located at Cheltenham, Prince
Georges County, His Honor the
Mayor of Baltimore, in company
with the above gentlemen, and others
of tlie committee from the Board
visited the above institution on Sat-
urday of last week. It was the
Mayor's first visit and he was much
pleased with it, and is determiaed to
do all in his power to increase
the appropriation from the city to-
wards this institution for its
proper and creditable support. The
Mayor freely expressed himself with
regard to many things in connection
with the management of the institu-
tion, and enthusiastically expressed
his very great interest in the boys.
In fact, his earnest address to the
boys in the chapel demonstrated that
he was not only a man of a great
head, but also of a warm, kindly
and sympathetic heart: Seldom have
we been so impressed with a speech
from a public official as we were
with the utterances of Mayor Hayes
He spoke right out from the heart.
The principles of the Mayor with
respect to that institution are sound
and have the ring, not of flunkeyism,
but of genuine manliness, Certainly
the colored people of Maryland have
a warm friend in the present Mayor
of Baltimore.
It is far better to have a head to
lose than not to have any head at
at all.
