Russian Trip Planned
It was further reported that
the Rev. Mr. Smith planned to
leave soon for Russia, where he
intends to make a study of Rus-
sia's attitude toward Christianity
and the colored race.
The Rev. Mr. Smith is the son
of Charles C. Smith, of Scranton,
Pa., and a brother of Miss Ruth.
Gwendolyn Smith, teacher at
Southern University, Scotland-

Accused Bishop Sims
Still in South Africa-
CAPETOWN, South Africa.-
(Special to ANP) -Bishop D. H.
Sims, of the African Methodist
Episcopal Church, told the adult
school at the Unitarian Church
here recently that "Education is
progressive participation in com-
plete living.
The A.M.E. prelate, who heads
the African division of his church
by authority vested in him by the
main body in America, is at pres-
ent the cynosure of all eyes be-
cause of the publicity turned on
him following accusations of de-
sertion and grave charges of im-
moriality, filed against him by
his wife with the A.M.E. tribunal
in the United States, recently.
Indications are that the bishop
will not obey the summons of the
A.M.E. board in the U.S. to re-
turn to face the charges against
him. It was pointed out that the
American churchman has won
quite a large following in South
Africa since he has been here.
