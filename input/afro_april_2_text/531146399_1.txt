Girl Killer
Tragedy Takes Place in
State Reform School.
BALTIMORE. Another chapter in a tragic trail
of juvenile delinquency and blighted youth was written
Friday when Roland McClellan, himself a girl-killer,
died at the Freedmen's Hospital with a fractured skull
and concussion of the brain.
When admitted to the
hospital, it was said, attend-
ants were told that he re-
ceived the injuries when he
fell down a flight of stairs
at the Cheltenham school of
reformation.
Another Version
Another version, which came
to the AFRO-AMERICAN, was
to the effect that young McClellan
received his fatal injuries as the
result of being hit on the head
with a baseball bat by another in-
mate, James Tynes. The boys
quarrelled, it was said, and Tynes
used the bat in the fight that
resulted.
Tynes is reported to have been
committed to the institution last
July. He was charged with the
theft of an automobile and with
running over and fatally wound-
ing a girl.
Killed His Sweetheart
McClellan was committed to
Cheltenham in May after
he had been convicted of killing
15-year-old Lillie May Cherry on
April 21 when she visited his
home.
Testimony at the trial was to
the effect that he and Miss
Cherry quarrelled over his atten-
tions to a Miss Dorothy Wilson, a
friend of the slain girl. Miss
Cherry went to his home on her
way to work at 7:00 a.m., wit-
nesses said.
He testified that Miss Cherry
approached him with a knife and
he grabbed a shot gun and fired.
The killing at the time caused
considerable interest because of
the sordidness of life it revealed.
Orphaned since 1929, the Cherry
girl had lived a precarious life
with relatives, it was said. Testi-
mony by police brought out that
before she was 15 years of age
she was visiting the home of Mc-
Clellan as his sweetheart.
Relatives fought a court battle
over the possession of her body
and insurance benefits.
