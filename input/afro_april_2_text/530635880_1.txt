That the House of Reforma-
tion for boys at Cheltenham
may undergo drastic changes in

result of an investigation made
this week by the Grand Jury is
the belief of those interested in
the work.
Recommendation for stricter State
supervision and possibly complete
State control may be the outcome,
according to some members of the
jury.
Witnesses Testify
The present investigation being
made into the affairs of this institu-
fon is the result of numerous let-
ters sent by inmates from time to
ime complaining of conditions
here.
In a recent letter to the Grand
Jury an inmate complained of cruel
and inhuman treatment. Besides
ack of proper food, long hours of
work the letter charged that inhu-
ran punishment was administered.
Gets Bread and Tea
Another witness before the. body
testified that bread and tea formed
the main diet for most of the time
and that the inmates were rarely
en meat except from time to time
in soup.
These complaints, it was pointed
out were in line with former charges
made to other grand juries in past
years..
Is Private Affair
Cheltenham is a private institu-
tion and under the supervision of a
private board of control. Under the
pesent arrangements the Juvenile
Court sends its wards there under
contract. They are given work at
the institution and at times farmed
out to work for others.
Former grand juries have made
Investigations and recommended
changes but to date none of these
recommendations have had any di-
rect effect.
Dr. George F. Bragg, Jr., a mem-
ber of the board of boverners, told

made acquainted with the charges
brought before the Grand Jury ex-
