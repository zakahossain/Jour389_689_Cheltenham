Hampton Grad
New Teacher
BALTIMORE
An appointment and a resig-
nation at the Cheltenham School
for Boys were announced during
a meeting of the school's Board
of Managers on Thursday.
Jacob Thomas Stewart, 25,
Hampton Institute graduate, has
been named instructor in tailor-
ing. He began his duties this
week. His appointment marks
the first time within recent years
that a colored person has been
named to teach tailoring.
The job carries with it a sal-
ary of $900 a year. Mr. Stewart
is a native of DeWitt, Va., but
has been residing in Riverhead,
N.Y., most of his life. He grad-
uated from Hampton in May,
1940.
White Supervisor Quits
The resignation was that of
O. C. Lott, white, recreational
supervisor. An examination will
be offered by the State employ-
ment commission to fill this
vacancy.
Morgan Will Be Host
to Bucknell Students
BALTIMORE
Twelve Bucknell College stu-
dents white, who are interested
in studying needs of colored
people in Baltimore, will visit the
city on Friday and Saturday,
March 21 and 22, at which time
they will be the guests of Mor-
gan State College.
Under the direction of the Rev.
Edward Carroll, chaplain of the
college, the students will go on
a tour of the city, including such
places as the Urban League,
NAACP, Edgar Allan Poe Homes,
the AFRO-AMERICAN and the
Vocational School.
The group will also attend ses-
sions of the Council of Negro
Organizatiins, which is holding a
two-day meeting the same week-
end.
