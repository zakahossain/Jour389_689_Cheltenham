Ran Away from Home,
Boys Arrested Here
Confessing that they had run
way from home, after being arrested
on suspicion, two boys, James Pope,
13, 4727 J Street, and Edward Dowd,
17, 906 47th Place, Washington, were
held pending return to their homes,
Tuesday.
The boys were taken into custody
when a policeman saw them walk-
ing on the Washington Boulevard
with hand bags. On being question-
ed, the boys told conflicting stories.
They finally admitted they had run
away with the intent of reaching
iNew York where they hoped to find
work.
The boys expressed a hope of be-
coming stage stars. The boys had
hitch-hiked from Washington to Bal-
timore. Their parents were unaware
of their escapade.
Seven Boys Committed
to Reform School
Facing accusations of policemen
that they had committed various of-
fenses, seven boys were committed to
the Cheltenham Reformatory School
after hearings before Judge Thomas
Waxter in the Juvenile Court, this
week.
Those who were committed to the
school were given periods ranging
from six months to three years. All
of the boys were second offenders.
