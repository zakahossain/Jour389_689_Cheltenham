Five Boys Sent to
Reform School
Facing accusations of participa-
tion in misdemeanors and felonies,
five boys were ordered committed
to Cheltenham School for Boys af-
ter hearings in the juvenile court,
this week.
The boys who were second of-
fenders, were also accused of vio-
lating paroles. They were: William
Hawkins, 15, 2259 Madison; Robert
Wyatt, 14, 652 Pitcher; Andrew
Parker, 15, 510 Druid Hill; Samuel
Brown, 13, Gastonia, N.C.; and
Richard Ford, 904 Aisquith.
Two Women Badly
Cut in Auto Mishap
Two women were badly cut
about the face and head when the
machine in which they were riding
on the Pimlico Road skidded and
crashed into a telegraph pole, Wed-
nesday.
The two who were Mrs. Mninie
Matthews, 1601 Springwood Ave-
nue, and Mrs. Mary Irvin, 1921 Rice
Street, Asbury Park, N.J., were
northbound. Both were cut on the
body.
