BALTIMORE
The Rev. Hyason Diggs, asso-
ciate pastor of Union Baptist Del. State Girls to
Church, has been commissioned
a second lieutenant and made a Get War Training

announced this week.
He will leave for Harvard Uni-
versity March 5 for a period of
orientation and indoctrination,
after which time he will be sent
to a permanent post.
Chaplain Diggs, a member of
the local Baptist Ministers' Con-
ference and of the National Bap-
tist Convention, Inc., did his col-
lege and seminary work at Vir-
ginia Union University.
He is a member of Kappa Alpha
Psi fraternity and was formerly
cottage master and chaplain at
Cheltenham School for Boys.
DOVER, Del.-Courses in en-
gineering, science and manage-
ment war training, under the U.S.
Office of Education, have been
started at Delaware State College.
The courses in chemistry, super-
vised by Dr. Robert S. Beale, di-
rector of the division on natural
sciences and mathematics, will
train college girls as laboratory
technicians in chemistry for war
industries.
University of Delaware faculty
members will conduct the courses
on the campus and a large number
of girls have already been en-
rolled.
