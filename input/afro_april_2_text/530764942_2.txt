One Race Delegate
at W.C.T.U. Meet
Baltimorean Attends National Con-
vention in Indianapolis.
The National W.C.T.U. convention
met at Indianapolis at which time
there were 1050 bona fide delegates,
one of whom was Mrs. Violet H.
Whyte, of this city, national field
worker among Negroes.
Prizes were awarded to members
whose records showed the most ef-
ficiency in their work. Two prizes
went to Mrs. Whyte, one medal for
securing a hich ner cent of Negro
members during the year; one ten-
dollar check for outstanding achieve-
ment along lines of social morality.
Addresses 5000
During the conference. Mrs. Whyte
addressed audiences at the churches,
the Murat Theatre, Crispus Attucks
High School and at the convention
hall where it was estimated that she
reached over 5000 people of both
races.
Statements of work accomplished
while in attendance in Indianapolis:
Addresses. Simpson M.E. Church, on
Law Observance: Mt. Perrin Baptist
Church, on W.C.T.U. Ideals; Murat
Theatre, on Educational Work Among
Negroes: Crispus Attucks High, on
the Meaning of Temperance and
How You May Help: Mass Meeting
at Club House, on Our New Mehods;
and addresses at points suburban to
the city. Asummary of the work
shows: six addresses, twenty confer-
ences with workers and forty-tyo
homes visited.

Young Woman Accuses
Mission Pastor
Miss Jennie Garrison Says Minister
Employer Threatened Her
Accused by Miss Jennie Garrison,
1041 N. Eutaw street, of threatenin
to do her bodily harm, the Reverend
John Smallwood, mission worker, 914
Eden street, was arraigned in the
Northeastern police station, Friday.
According to testimony testimony presented
at the hearing, the Reverend Mr.
Smallwood had made several threats
against the young woman, who is in
charge of his mission at Harford ave-
nue and Madison street, on October
2, when she failed to do as he had
ordered.
The following day he ordered her
to scrub several floors which she re-
fused to do. During an altercation
which folloyed the preacher is alleg
to have threatened her and attempt-
ed to strike her also. Miss Garrison
told the court that the trouble is a
result of her repulsing his advances
several days previous.
Rev. Smallwood was dismissed of
the charges after being warned by
Magistrate O'Neill.
Five Boys Sentenced in
Beating Case
Five boys, under 17, William Cook,
706 Vine street, Irvin Porter, 6017
Pearce street, George Similley, 16,
565 Josephine street. and two others,
were given two years in Cheltenham
for assaulting and brutally beating
John Nathan, 311 N. Pearce street.
Monday.
Cook and Porter had charges
pending against them in Juvenile!
court when they were arraigned on
this charge.
The victim, John Nathan. was an
elderly man and had to be carried
from court as a result of the beat-
ing.
