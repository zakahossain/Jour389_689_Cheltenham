BEER HEARING
With the Rev. Ernest Lyon, pas-
tor of Ames M.E. Church, acting as
the star witness, Clarence Webb,
32, 1235 W. Lafayette Avenue was
held for violating the new beer law,
Sunday.
Webb was arrested by Sergeant
Edward Hitzelberger at 1:25 a.m.
after the minister had complained
to the Northwestern district about
the noise in the rear of his home.
Officers testified that when the
house at 828 Woodyear Street which
is operated by Webb, was raided, the
man was seen selling beer to
number of persons.

SIX BOYS SENT TO
CHELTENHAM
Facing the accusations of police
that they had committed misde-
meanors, six boys were sent to the
Cheltenham Reform School after
trials in the juvenile court, this
week.
The boys who were accused and
sent to the school were: Frank
Johnson, 14, 1650 Woodyear Street;
Raymond Cross, 12, 612 Fremont
Avenue; Howard Thomas, 14, 1531
Woodyear Street; Sylvester Wil-
liams 14. 1636 Bruce Street, and
James Boone, 16, 1609 Mulliken
Street, and Prince Edward, 17, Flor-
ence, S.C.
Woman Held by Cops
As Shoplifter
Charges of shoplifting were made
against Mrs. Mildred Sessons 23,
1012 Madison Avenue when she was
arraigned in the Western Police
Court, Saturday.
Mrs. Bertha Jellers, a female de-
tective at the May Department store
stated that she saw the woman
take a pair of garters. When she
was searched two dresses, two pairs
of socks, two pair of hose, a can of
Kleenall and the garters were found
in a bag. Mrs. Sessons was held
for the action of the grand jury.
