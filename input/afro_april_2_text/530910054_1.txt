Contrary to Attitude.
"Mr. Grasty's statement," said Dr.
Scott, "does not indicate when he
received the advice from Mr. Wash-

Often Misquoted.
"Often during his lifetime, Mr.
Washington was misquoted by per-
sons who themselves, wishing to con-
tinue situations excluding colored
people from activity in connection
with their own schools and institu-
tions, quoted him as favoring their
notions. I cannot make this state-
ment with respect to Mr. Grasty, but
I do know that Mr. Washington was
constantly writing letters refuting
alleged statements of his of a simi-
