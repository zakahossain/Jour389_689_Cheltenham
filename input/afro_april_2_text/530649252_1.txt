WALTER GREEN POST
HITS SCHOOL SYSTEM
Resolutions Take Slap At
County Education And
Cheltenham
The Maryland Department of
the American Legion now in its
ninth annual convention at Eas-
ton, Md., is expected to act on
two resolutions submitted by the
Walter Green Post number 14
this week.
The resolutions crawn up by Cap-
tain Milton Dorsey and Edward H.
Marshall are to the effect that the
convention go on record as indorsing
a uniform length of school year for all
children in the counties, adequate
establishment of high schools when
the law is satisfied, and a uniform
salary scale for all teachers.
The following grievances are cited
in the resolution:
a.-The maximum length of the
school year for colored boys and giris
In our counties is only eight (8)
months.
b-These children have no high
schools in many of the counties, Bal-
timore County, for an example. The
law, however, in this case states. That
a high school may be established
wherever there is a minimum of
fifteen (15) elementary graduates:
Baltimore County had 89 graduates
last semester.
c-The maximum salaries for coi-
ored county teachers is only $70.
HIT CHELTENHAM
The second resolution points out the
evils at Cheltenham Reform School.
According the finding of a committee
only 30 days' schooling is given to the
boys during the year.
There are no trades taught these.
boys, except baking, broom-making,
and farming.
The boys are hired out under the
convict-lease system to the surround-
ing farmers who act as guards with
pay in meny cases.
No supervision is given the conduct.
instruction, and treatment of these
boys.
No effort is made to reclaim these
boys to citizenship.
These boys are released under a
poor system, since no notification of
the release is sent the parent or guar-
dian, the transportation is usually left;
in the hands of an agreeable passer-
by who drops off the released boys
anywhere in the designated vicinity.
The instiution is privately owned
and controlled with the help of a
State appropriation of '$53.310. an-
nually.
The post asks the convention to
draw up a petition favoring state con-
trol of this institution and recom-
mends that measures be taken to rec-
tify existing conditions..
