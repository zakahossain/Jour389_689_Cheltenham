MEDIA, Pa. Clarence Guy, a senior
in the Media High School, Media, Pa.,
is the most proficient student of Latin
that has ever graced the halls of the
school,
2.
Such is the statement made by the
principal, Dr. W. H. Michaels, white,
in a recent address before the Chey-
ney Community League, of which, Dr.
Leslie P. Hill is president. The Latin
papers of the other students are pass-,
ed to Guy for examination.
As well as being a student of Latin,
Guy has proved his ability as an ora-
tor, having won first prizes in contests
sponsored by the Media Branch of
the National Association for the Ad-
vancement of Colored People, and the
Lodge of Elks,
He will enter the state and national
contests sponsored by the Elks. He is
19 years of age, and a dependable
player on the football and baseball
teams of the high school.
It might be added that Guy is pro-
ficient in all of his other studies as
well, only "A" and "A plus" appearing
on his report during the year. He
plans to enter Harvard University in
the fall.

CHICAGO 'EXPELS'
TWO BALTO. LADS
CHICAGO.-The wicked old city of
Chicago is too good for Rudolph Jones
and Jasper Johnson, both Baltimore
boys.
Somewhere between here and ten
miles out of that city they are sup-
posed to be wending their "back, back,
back to Baltimore," for the Windy
City will have no more of them.
In Crook Clean-up
Johnson, who was once an inmate
of Cheltenham Reform School and
Jones, who "did time' 'at Mays Sta-
tion, ran afoul of the law last week
when Johnson went over to call on
Jones, and finding him away, forced
the door of an adjoinig room and took
a hat and one dollar. After cutting
the hat down to "English" style,
Johnson was sporting the newly ac-
quired lid when he met Jones.
Immediately his one time pal rec-
ognized the top piece and demanded
that it be given him so he could re-
turn it to the owner. To this John-
son did not agree, and by the time
the police reached the scene, Johnson
had divided the suit Jones was wear-
ing into several equal parts with a
razor.
When the two were haled before the
Judge his sentence was "beat it." A
police car took the youths to the ten-
mile limit and dumped them out with
another reminder of what the Judge
had told them.
