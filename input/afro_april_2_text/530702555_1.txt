To the Editor:
No longer a member of the Board of Mana-
gers of the House of Reformation, I desire, as
I may have leisure, to review some of the phases
of the work of that institution. In this. I shall
only indicate the particular incident which first
fixed my attention upon that institution.
During the year 1898 I was the publisher
and Editor of "The Ledger," a weekly publica-
tion of this city. At that time both the city and
state were under Republican rule. Lloyd
Lowndes was the Governor, and William T.
Malster, was Mayor.
In the issue of "The Ledger" March 12, 1898,
there was this Editorial:
*
*
THE MISTAKE OF MAYOR MALSTER
Some weeks ago the Mayor of Baltimore ap-
3pointed two white citizens as the city's mana-
gers of the Board of the Cheltenham House of
Reformation for Colored Boys. As soon as the
appointments were announced, through the press,
the attention of Mayor Malster was called to
the seeming humiliation which such an act nec-
essarily implied with respect to the many qualifi-
ed and capable colored citizens of this communi-
ty. The Mayor left the impression upon the gen-
tlemen who directed his attention to the matter,
that he would most gladly rectify the mistake
at some convenient time. To our great amaze-
ment and surprise, we were informed during the
present week. through the daily press, that one
of the appointees had resigned, and immediate-
ly following this information is the statement
that the Mayor had appointed a prominent white
politician in his stead, if we mistake not, a
gentleman who is a WHISKEY DEALER.

of a House of Reformation for Colored Boys, but
