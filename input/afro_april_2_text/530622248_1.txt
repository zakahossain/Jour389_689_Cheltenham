Seorges County is back in the spot.

This time it is because its secretary,
Mr. W. H. Janney, refuses to per-

phia, to see or communicate with
her brother committed there by or-
er of Baltimore City courts several
years ago.
The solution of this case is simple
nough. Mrs. Jones has only to ap-
ply for a writ of habeas corpus
through any local attorney and
Cheltenham authorities will be com-
pelled to permit her to see her bro-
ther. In every single case in which
habeas corpus has been asked courts
have also returned these boys to
their parents and guardians when
requested.
The solution of the whole Chel-
tenham problem is not so easy. The
Institution is dominated by natives
of Prince. Georges County who are
not experts in reform themselves
and did not lister to the Mayor's
committee last year which recom-
mended certain changes.
As the result, boys there are mis-
erably treated, trained little, if at
all, and farmed out like convicts
to work on neighboring plantations.
If there is any place worse than
Cheltenham for cildren, we do not
know of it..
Here is a matter for the Grand
Jury to go into again. The city
should be requested to withhold its
appropriation to Cheltenham, until
its trustees reform it.
