CRUELTY CHARGED
Cheltenham Boys Committee
On Fair Treatment Replies
To Dr. Bragg
That each of the 200 boys at
Cheltenham House of Reforma-
tion gets one and one-half
pounds of beef and five pounds
of pork monthly, is claimed in
a letter from a "committee of
fair treatment" at that place.
The letter, which was written in
teaponse to a communication ap-
pearing in these columns recently
from, the pen of the Rev. Dr. George
FBragg, sets out in detail the
monthly list of provisions bought for
the 1200 boys in the institution.
According to this list each boy
gets one and one-half pounds of
beef each month. This is the equiv-
salent of dividing a one-pound steak
among 20 boys once each day. To
this is added a monthly allowance
of five pounds of pork meat each
month or a total meat ration for
each boy of one-fifth of a pound
each day.
The monthly diet sheet sent by
this committee includes 1200 loaves
of bread, 75 pans of corn bread, 50
buahels of beans, 18 cart loads of
cabbages, 25 bags string beans, 24
cart loads of greens, 225 pounds of
oats, 1042 pounds of pork, 300
pounds of beef, 180 gallons of milk,
15 bags of potatoes, 15 bags of tur-
nips, 30 bags of sweet potatoes, 15
boxes of prunes, peaches and apples.
Wants Race Teachers
The letter from the committee is
as follows:
We, the committee on fair treat-
ment, have read Dr. Bragg's letter
about having teachers of our own
color here. That would be much
better than ex-bartenders and old
Lime topers and some one who can-
not read themselves, not capable
of ruling themselves, rauch less
others.
What the manager means by a
born disciplinarian must be one of
the family who is a dive keeper so
he can hold his job as county poll-
tician and will be recommended
by any old bartender or brandy-
wine rum slinger.
Have two now at the head. They
had to be reformed first before they
were elevated.
What the Home wants is bright,
intelligent and kind bosses at the
hand and no county brutes.
Last week, Freddy Smith, a small
boy, always quiet, and good
natured, but the nervous and
hysterical woman had him lashed
40 lashes but the big stiffs who can
kick up h........ only a few lashes.
Runny lashed a little boy in
the laundry under Mr. Longfelt's
bedroom while he was sick and you
could hear the child scream all
over the Institution. Why don't you
brutes try some of that treatment
on us large boys. But what can you
expect of the unintelligent coun-
try rum-slingers?
There are some good officers
down here who treat us right. Mr.
Longfelt is one of thee best and al-
way's gives us good advice. He
knows how hard we have to work
and sees that we get a plenty of
wholesome food.
But if one little boy speaks out
in the dining room she knocks
out the whole school for one viola-
tion.
Send us a boss down here who
will not work us from dark to
dark or hire us out from 4 in the
morning to 9 or 10 at night. They
regard us as "only niggers."
What the Home needs, as Mr.
Bragg says, are two good Balti-
more men and white men at the
head who should be examined as
for qualifications and character.
The county managers don't care
for us, all they care is to
get a job for a man to hold his
vote. They never come here to
visit.
