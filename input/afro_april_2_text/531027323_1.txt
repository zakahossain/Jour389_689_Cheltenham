8 BOYS SENT TO
REFORM SCHOOL
Given hearings on charges rang-
ing from disorderly conduct to burg-
lary, eight boys were ordered sent
to the Cheltenham Reformatory,
following hearings before Judge
Thomas Waxter in Juvenile court,
last week.
With seven charges of burglary
placed against him by Northern
District police, William Wills, 14,
said by officers to be the leader of
youthful gang, 2827 Simpson Street,
was sentenced to the school for a
period of two years.
The other boys who were sent to
the school for a varied number of
viclations were: Paul Clayton, 17,
Easton, Md.; William Wright, 12,
1302 Mulligan Street: Fred Logan,
15, of 419 Bethel Street; Bernard
Palmer, 11, of 910 W. Franklin
Street; Howard Ornder, 13 of 712
Sharp; Ivan Queen, 16, of 109 W.
Fayette Street and Daniel Scott, 515
N. Calhoun Street.

TOMS RIVER
By MISS NANCY WORTHY
10 Centre Street
TOMS RIVER, N. J.-The Rev. George
Crawley preached at the Second Baptist
Church, Sunday night.
Mrs. Mary Alexander left Thursday for
her home in Philadelphia where she will
spend the winter.
The Ror. T. Tillman preahced at the
Well's Chapel, Sunday night.
Miss Bennie Lee Meadows, Forker Riv-
fer, spent the week-end with her brother,
Albert Meadows, of Centre Street.
Mrs. Mack Worthy, who has been ill
is recovering.
The Daughter Elks gave their annual
memorial program at the Well's Chapel
Sunday night. Rev. I. Tilman preached
the sermon. Annie Payne was mistress
of ceremonies. There were solos by Miss
Myrtle Pinkney and Mrs. Julia Young.
Mrs. Mac Roe and Isaac rendered piano
solos, also remarks b the daughter ruler,
Mrs. Lizzie James.
