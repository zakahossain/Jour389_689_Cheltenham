Cop Sługging Charge
to Be Investigated
BALTIMORE-
An order for an investigation into the slugging
of Charles Tyler, 30, of 11 S. Regester Street, by Pa-
trolman Louis H. Schultz of the Northeastern district
on Sunday, was ordered by Inspector Thomas Moon-
ey, Tuesday afternoon.
The officer reported that he placed Tyler under
arrest at 1530 Ashland Avenue, for a minor disorder.
While en route to a patrol box, Tyler struck him in
the face, knocking him to his knees, the officer stated.
To protect himself, it was necessary to use his
espantoon, the officer declared. Tyler was taken to
the Johns Hopkins Hospital for treatment of lacera-
tions of the scalp..
Found Reefers in
Tavern, School
Boy Tells Court
BALTIMORE-
Charged with the possession of
narcotics when six reefers were
found in his pockets, Edward
Sampson, 17, Douglass High
School student, of 700 700 Dolphin
Street, was held for the grand
jury, Friday.
The youth, police say, was found
in a tavern with the doped cigar-
ettes in a small tin case. His ar-
rest was made after a plainclothes
officer saw him pass something to
another youth, it was testified.
Under Suspicion
For several weeks, officers
stated, the youth has been under
suspicion as a member of a gang
that peddled the reefers. Patrol-
man John Brown testified that
Sampson has been arrested previ-
ously for a similar offense.
The high school student testi-
fied that he found the cigarettes
while in a Pennsylvania Avenue
tavern. He denied statements
that he passed cigarettes to anoth-
er youth.

5 TO CHELTENHAM
Convicted on charges of mis-
demeanors and felonies, five boys
ranging from 14 to 16 years of age
were committed to the Chelten-
ham School of Reformation after
hearings in the Juvenile Court,
this week.
The youths, police testified, were
second offenders and each was on
probation when arrested recently.
