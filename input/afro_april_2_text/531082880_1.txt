Morgan Receives
$45,000 State
Aid in Budget
(By Staff Correspondent)
Excitement
ANNAPOLIS.
burned at a fever heat in this
city, Wednesday, following the re-
lease of the governor's budget of
$60,000,000 that includes an allot-
ment of $45,000 for Morgan Col-
lege.
_ This amount marks an increase
of $21,600 in the fund provided for
the college, but is $30,000 short of
the $75,000 requested.
Persons close to the governor
assert that the shortage can be
explained by agreements that are
to be made with Lincoln Univer-
sity in Pennsylvania and Howard
University in Washington, where-
by the tuition of Maryland stu-
dents will be paid until a state uni-
versity can be set up.
$59,000 for Henryton
It has also been indicated by the
administration that the sum of
$59,000 requested for new build-
ings at Henryton, for the housing
and care of tuberculosis victims,
will be taken care of in the pro-
posed bond issue of $855,000.
The proposed budget includes
an appropriation of $20,000 for
Cheltenham, but activity is now
under way to make this institution
entirely under state supervision
which will mean a necessary in-
crease in the appropriation.
On Wednesday a special com-
mittee from the Cheltenham board
visited the governor here, and its
members were told that the insti-
tutino must be taken over by the
state.
Members Dubious
The committee presented itself
following intimations by some of
the board members that the state
might not be willing to assume
control of the institution-as a
number of organizations and civic
groups have demanded.
The Maryland School for the
Blind, which requested an appro-
priation of $13,000, was granted
its wish, and this amount repre-
sents no increase over that given
last year.
No increase was given the N. M.
Carroll Home of the M.E. Church,
which receives its customary al-
lotment of $5,000.
The sum of $500 was allotted to
the Shelter for the Aged and In-
firm, which failed to ask for and
did not receive an increase in its
appropriation.
A.M.E.'s Get $1500
Another static figure in the bud-
get is the sum of $1500 for the
A.M.E. Home for the Aged, which
did not ask for an increase over
the amount that it received last
year.
St. Elizabeth's Home sought an
increase of $500 over its appro
priation of $12,000 received last
year which was granted.
Other institutions failing to ask
for or receive increases in their
appropriations are St. Mary's
Home for Boys, $1250; St. Cath-
erine's Home for Girls, $1250; and
the House of Good Shepherd,
$4,000.
Princess Anne Cut
Princess Anne Academy and
Bowie State Normal School were
given upsets. The former asked
that its allowance be increased
from $15,672 to $17,672, but was
given a decreased amount of $15,-
513. The latter sought $44,385
instead of its last year's sum of
$38,069, but was given $37,411.
Crownsville, seeking $245,000,
receives $123,950, but will be re-
duced to $115,700 in 1937.
The Good Shepherd Hospital, a
new institution on the list, will re-
ceive $5,000.
The Henry Watson Children's
Aid Society, seeking $15,000 in-
stead of its allotment of $8,000, re-
ceived $9,000, while the St. Peter
Claver Industrial School, request-
ing a $500 increase in its allotment
of $3,000. had its wish granted.
Asylum Gets Increase
St. Vincent's Infant Asylum,
seeking a $500 increase in its al-
lotment of $14,500, was given that
amount, which brings its total to
$15,000.
Provident Hospital, which faced
a huge deficit because of the failure
of state funds to meet the amount
of money expended on state pa-
tients, was refused a request for
$16,000. The amount alloted,
which was $14,000, is an increase
of $2,000 over previous allotments.
