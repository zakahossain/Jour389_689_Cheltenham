No Menacing Reds.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
Fruit has been borne from the
seeds sown by your editorial of
some months ago, entitled "No
Menacing Reds," which said in
part:
"In fact, there is more real
Christianity among white Com-
munists than in the white YMCA,
the white Christian Endeavor So-
cieties or the white so-called
Christian churches."
Since this editorial was pub-
lished, the Communists have
been instrumental in organizing
both colored and white share-
croppers into a union which now
has a membership of ten thous-
and and which has helped in bet-
tering working conditions and
wages.
The case of Angelo Herndon,
the Scottsboro case, defense of
Ethiopia, etc., are just a few of
the many undertakings.
S. CARMAN
1204 E. Baltimore Street
Baltimore, Md.

Red Cross Snubs Ethiopia.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
The International Red Cross has
refused to give Ethiopia its ser-
vices, and will leave the wound-
ed of Abyssinia to rot and die in
the jungles of Africa.
Non-combatant, neutral, medi-
cal, surgical and nursing units
are recognized as necessary forces
of mercy for soldiers wounded and
sick in war zones and are accepted
civilized forces.
DR. JOSEPH T. THOMAS.
2406 E. 40th Street,
Cleveland, Ohio.
F. D.'s Alarm Clock.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
In a letter to the President re-
cently, I said:
"It seems so inconsiderate for
those seventy erstwhile, and pos-
sible future, enemies of our land
and people to come in here on a
visit and then try to make a con-
trast of the German-Jewish situ-
ation with that of the races in
our America.
"Just why, too, are Mr. Hitler's
seventy hired henchmen-doctors
traveling by numbers instead of
names? Are you asleep?"
MOSES ST. MATTHEW ASHLEY
Long Island City N.Y.
School to Replace Cheltenham Is
Urged.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
Since white officials have re-
fused to allow the State of Mary-
land to take over control of the
Cheltenham Reformatory, I sug-
gest that religious, civic, and
fraternal organizations unite in
the erection of an institution for
delinquent boys.
This school could be organized
with the proper backing of our
leading citizens throughout the
State. I have an ideal site, 209
acres, adjoining Crownsville State
Hospital.
I am willing to have a school
and cottages erected here, so that
misguided boys may have proper
spiritual, educational, and indus-
trial training to prepare them for
good citizenship.
MAUD EDGELL.
Waterbury, Md.
