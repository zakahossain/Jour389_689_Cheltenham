GABBIN
By THE GHOST
NEW YORK CITY
The first and largest Easter partee was
that given by Cecella and Mabel Dudley.
The second was that of Norma Potts, and
last, but not least, Eddie (Comites) Parkus.
Have you met Barrett Johnson's two
cousins, the Smith boys?,? Frances (Am-
sterdam News) Ball, maybe you forgot to
mention that Betty (Jr. Deb.) Arrington
refuses to go anywhere without Warren
Hollings, and Billy Garvin does not go to
Columbia, and Frankie (Comites) Bourne
is not an Elite.
THINGS I CAN'T SEE
Why Evelyn Bryant sticks to David
Brown when he's just gaga over Eurtha
Edwards.
Why Julia Summersett pulled that fast
one in the Café Royal and then took a
week-end trip?
Why Muriel Southerland refuses to have
a boy friend'and spends most of her time
staying at home reading?
Why the Junior Debs are only taking in
three members, which, by the way, is a
violation of their quota.
MYSTERIES AND OTHER TALK
:
Philip (Budy) Hilton moved to bachelor
quarters.
I have to smile whenever I see Leonard
(Comites) Jones clown...He's good.
Leo Terry has been nicknamed "Mouse-
o" by the boys.
I wonder if Teddy Mays has a new
heart beat? Clement Carrington is in
South America.
The Issac Jacksons (Evelyn Wiggens)
have just greeted Sir Stork.
And Dame Rumor has it that Buster
Jacobs is engaged to Grace Peyton.
The 29 Club of Brooklyn, called so be-
cause it has twenty-nine members, is
throwing its annual shake in May.
Howard Blackston, àce relay man for
Boys' High, spent the whole week-end in
the nation's capital.
Johnye-Pope Mitchel and Winnie Mason
are doing their best to make that stage-
coach party a success.
Ernie Stoken came in for the holidays
to take Elizabeth (Jr. Deb.) Johnson
places.
Jimmie (Brown) Kingsland came into
town and then went down to D.C.
A few of the Guardsmen are preparing
to go South, to a formal to be given by
the Baltimore chapter in that vicinity.
Didi Walterding will not go places when
her heart-throb, Buster Davis, is not in
town.
Arlene Barker gave a nice freeble at
her house on Wednesday last, and this
brought about a new romance between
Constance (Brooklyn Deb) Clark and
Harry (Skeeter) Hollings.
The boys call Aida (Red Pepper) Bear-
den. "Lady Dunkwell."
And Doris Carter, "Duchess Mothball."
That Doris Bly-Edgar Cheltenham affair
looks serious.
I wonder what's happened to Dorothy
Douglas, Edna Mitchell, and Doris Madden.
Donald Carrington doesn't know it yet
but he nearly broke up a perfect- friend-
ship between two guys and two gals.
Wyetta Hill could save a lot of trouble
by explaining why she didn't come up for
the holidays.
Jimmy (Guardsmen) Adams plans to at-
tend N. Y. U. when he finishes Lincoln.
Ask Elaine Walterding to show you that
new set of photos she had made.
Hope Davis has been seeing a lot of
Leonard Jones and it's all done when Ann
Lewis Motley isn't looking, too.
Kenneth James of Manhattan comes
over this-a-way a lot and Mary Burwell is
the cause of it all.
Barney Hairston and Vera Trice are
slated to be married in June.

Arthur Burwell and Unis Presley have
