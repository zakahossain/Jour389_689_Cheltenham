RITCHIE ASKS
$47,500 TO AID
INSTITUTIONS
$8,000 Increase for the
Charity Relief Work
in State.
PROVIDENT MAY
GET $17,000
-
$5,000 More than was
Granted in 1932.
A total of $47,500 for colored in-
stitutions has been recommended!
to Governor Albert C. Ritchie to
be included in the state budget
for 1933 by the Board of State Aid
and Charities, it was learned this
week.
This amount carries an increase
of $8,000 in the recommendation
for the awards this year over the
amounts granted last year.
Of this amount, $17,000 has been
asked for Provident Hospital and
if included in the state budget and
then passed by the state legisla-
ture, the institution will receive the
amount recommended. This is an
increase of $5,000 over the $12,000
granted by the state to Provident
Hospital last year.
Cheltenham Gets $20,000
The boys' reform school at Chel-
tenham, Md., will receive the
highest amount if the recommen-
dation of $20,000 stands the test of
fitting into the budget. No in-
crease for this school has been ask-
led.
It was recommended to the board
in a letter that a colored social
worker be placed at Cheltenham.
The matter will be discussed in a
later meeting, it was said at the
offices of the board, Thursday.
The Good Shepherd Hospital has
been recommended a sum of $1,500
with no increase asked over last
year.
Asks Three Increases
In the two other cases of colored
institutions the St. Anthony Or-
phanage will receive a total of
$4,000, an increase of $1,000 and
the James Cardinal Gibbons In-
Istitute has been recommended to
receive a total of $5,000, with a
$2,000 increase.
