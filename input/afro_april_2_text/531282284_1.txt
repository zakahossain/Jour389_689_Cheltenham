The Whirling
Hub
By GLADYS PHILLIANS
ENTERTAINMENT
All the time we hear about
the movies, the plays, the
dances, the parties, the con-
certs, the lectures, the art
exhibits, the sports, the
clubs, the churches, the
schools, the libraries, the
museums, the parks, the
gardens, the zoos, the restau-
rants, the hotels, the stores,
the streets, the buildings, the
bridges, the tunnels, the sub-
ways, the trains, the buses,
the planes, the ships, the
cars, the trucks, the taxis, the
bikes, the scooters, the skate-
boards, the roller skates, the
ice skates, the skis, the snow-
mobiles, the boats, the canoes,
the kayaks, the rafts, the
swimming pools, the beaches,
the lakes, the rivers, the
oceans, the mountains, the
deserts, the forests, the fields,
the farms, the ranches, the
cities, the towns, the villages,
the hamlets, the countries, the
continents, the planets, the
stars, the galaxies, the uni-
verse, the everything else.
We are surrounded by so
much that we can't possibly
take it all in. We have to
choose what we want to see,
what we want to do, what we
want to be. We have to make
decisions about what is impor-
tant to us, what is meaningful
to us, what is worthwhile to
us. We have to find our own
way in the world, our own
path, our own purpose.
This is not easy. It is a
constant struggle, a constant
challenge, a constant test. We
are always being bombarded
with information, with opin-
ions, with suggestions, with
demands. We are always
being told what we should do,
what we should think, what we
should feel. We are always
being pressured to conform, to
obey, to follow.
But we must resist this
pressure. We must be true to
ourselves. We must listen to
our own hearts, our own
minds, our own souls. We
must follow our own dreams,
our own passions, our own
visions.
This is not selfish. It is
necessary. If we do not live
our own lives, who will? If
we do not express our own
selves, who will? If we do not
fulfill our own destinies, who
will?
We are all unique. We all
have something to offer the
world. We all have something
to contribute to the human
experience. We all have
something to share with each
other.
So let us not be afraid to
be ourselves. Let us not be
afraid to stand out from the
crowd. Let us not be afraid to
be different.
Let us be proud of who we
are. Let us be proud of what
we do. Let us be proud of
what we believe.
Let us be ourselves, fully
and completely. Let us be
ourselves, without apology
and without regret. Let us be
ourselves, now and forever.
* * *
The world is a wonderful
place. It is full of beauty,
full of wonder, full of possi-
bility. It is a place where
anything can happen, where
anything is possible, where
anything can be achieved.
But it is also a dangerous
place. It is full of violence,
full of hatred, full of despair.
It is a place where people are
hurt, where people are killed,
where people are destroyed.
We must be aware of both
the good and the bad in the
world. We must be aware of
the beauty and the ugliness,
the joy and the sorrow, the
hope and the despair.
We must not be naive. We
must not be blind. We must
not be ignorant.
We must be informed. We
must be educated. We must
be aware.
We must also be active. We
must not be passive. We must
not be indifferent.
We must do something to
make the world a better place.
We must do something to
help those who are in need.
We must do something to
fight against injustice.
We must do something to
promote peace.
We must do something to
protect the environment.
We must do something to
preserve our culture.
We must do something to
advance our civilization.
We must do something to
make the world a better place
for all.
This is not easy. It is a
constant struggle, a constant
challenge, a constant test. But
it is worth it. It is worth
fighting for. It is worth dying
for.
The world is a wonderful
place. Let us make it even
better.
* * *
The future is uncertain. We
do not know what will happen
tomorrow, next week, next
year, or in the next century.
We do not know what new
technologies will be devel-
oped, what new discoveries
will be made, what new
challenges will be faced.
We do not know what new
wars will be fought, what new
diseases will be spread, what
new disasters will occur.
We do not know what new
opportunities will arise, what
new possibilities will emerge,
what new dreams will be
realized.
But we can be sure of one
thing: the future will be
different from the present.
The world will change.
People will change.
Everything will change.
We must be prepared for
this change. We must be
ready to adapt to new circum-
stances, to new challenges, to
new opportunities.
We must be flexible. We
must be adaptable. We must
be resilient.
We must also be creative.
We must be innovative. We
must be imaginative.
We must be able to think
outside the box, to see things
in new ways, to come up with
new solutions.
We must also be optimistic.
We must believe in the future.
We must believe in ourselves.
We must believe in the
power of human ingenuity.
We must believe that we can
make the world a better place.
The future is uncertain. But
it is also full of promise. Let
us embrace it with open arms.
* * *
The present is all we have.
We cannot change the past.
We cannot predict the future.
All we have is the present
moment.
We must make the most of
it. We must live each day to
the fullest. We must savor
every moment.
We must appreciate the
beauty of the world around
us. We must appreciate the
love of our friends and family.
We must appreciate the
simple things in life.
We must also be grateful for
what we have. We must not
take anything for granted. We
must be thankful for our
health, our happiness, our
freedom.
We must also be mindful of
others. We must be kind to
those who are in need. We
must be compassionate to
those who are suffering. We
must be generous to those who
are less fortunate than us.
The present is all we have.
Let us make the most of it.
* * *
The past is gone. We cannot
change it. We cannot undo it.
We can only learn from it.
We can learn from our
mistakes. We can learn from
our successes. We can learn
from the mistakes and suc-
cesses of others.
We must not dwell on the
past. We must not let it
define us. We must not let it
hold us back.
We must move on. We must
look forward. We must focus
on the future.
The past is gone. Let us
learn from it and move on.
* * *
The future is coming. We
cannot stop it. We cannot
avoid it. We can only prepare
for it.
We must be ready for
anything. We must be ready
for change. We must be ready
for the unknown.
We must also be hopeful.
We must believe in the future.
We must believe in ourselves.
We must believe in the
power of human ingenuity.
The future is coming. Let
us be ready for it.
* * *
The present is now. It is
all we have. Let us make the
most of it.
* * *
The past is gone. Let us
learn from it.
* * *
The future is coming. Let
us be ready for it.
* * *
The present is now. Let us
live it to the fullest.
* * *
The past is gone. Let us
not forget it.
* * *
The future is coming. Let
us not fear it.
* * *
The present is now. Let us
love it.
* * *
The past is gone. The future
is coming. The present is
now. Let us live, learn, and
love.
