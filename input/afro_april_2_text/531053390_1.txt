ANNAPOLIS, Md. (Special) -
Taking into account that he had
a legislature with only 24 Republi-
cans and 94 Democrats in the
House and 11 Republicans and 17
Democrats in the Senate, Governor
Harry W. Nice felt that he had
done very well this week, as the
legislature adjourned.
Twenty-four Republicans in the
House voted selidly for his 17 ma-
jor bills, he said.
But in the Senate five members
did everything to defeat the Re-
publican program. It was not
done maliciously, he said, but it
was done. The employment
agency bill, carrying an appro-
priation of $40,000 which would
have been supplemented by a Fed-
eral grant, he said, was defeated
by Senator Phoebus (Rep. Somer-
set County).
It would have meant four posi-
tions in every county and 25 in
Baltimore City.
Auto Tax Reduced
Despite the $4,000,000 deficit
left by ex-Governor Albert C.
Ritchie, the governor said he had
been able to keep the tax rate at
the same level and to reduce the
automobile tax from $8.30 to $5,20.
The Democratic legislature kill-
ed the proposition that would
make $2 tags possible. Chauffeurs
will not be required to renew
their driving licenses every year
jas formerly.
The governor listed among his
19 objectives obtained the follow-
ing:
1. Increased appropriation of
$20.000 for Morgan College.
2. Ten thousand dollars to take
college and professional courses in
or outside the state by persons who
are denied attendance at the Uni-
versity of Maryland.
3. Six thousand dollars for a
commission to study means of
making higher education available
to all people.
Coroner Appointed
4. Appointment of Dr. George
S. Allen as coroner in Baltimore,
and appointment of several jus-
tices of the peace. In the case of
Mrs. Lula Powell, white, who
was objected to as a justice of the
peace, the governor said, he asked
that her name be withdrawn and
he will give her a recess point-
ment this week.
The governor also stated:
5. "The bill to equalize teachers'
salaries was lost, but I hope for
at least a five per cent increase
for colored as well as white
teachers from the 'Cushion Fund.'"
Only 5 Rejected
8. "Of 3,000 appointments only
five were rejected."
7. "The bill to take over the
House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham as a state in-
stitution went through. Senator
Melvin Fine (Rep., 4th Dist.)
certainly worked hard on it."
8. "I got a sum for the repair
of the executive mansion."
9. "A sum to make a survey of
state institutions;
10. "A law establishing pensions
for the aged;
11. "A three-man conservation
board;
12. "A fireman accident com-
mission.
13. "An engineering department
in the State Roads Commission."
Other accomplishments were:
14. Recodification of military
laws of the state.
$100,000 to Morgan
15. A law authorizing the state
to purchase Princess Anne Acad-
emy from Morgan College at a
cost of $100,000, to be paid in
annual installments of $25,000.
16. Twenty-four bills recodi-
fying the banking laws.
17. "A yellow dog law" for or-
ganized labor.
18. Separation of state police
from auto commissioner's office.
19. Law establishing the right
to appeal de novo and to give ad-
ditional testimony in awards by
accident commission.
Made Friends
Asked whether he had expect-
ted to get this far with a so-called
hostile legislature, Governor Nice
said he certainly had, that he had
not found it difficult to make
friends in the legislature, and
through these friends to smooth
out difficulties.
The governor said that at the
end of this term he did not ex-
pect to run again. When it was
suggested that he was now tired
out by day and night conferences
ture and might change his mind
during the 90 days of the legisla-
after he was rested, Governor
Nice said:
"My mind is made up now. I
Jam not going to run again."
