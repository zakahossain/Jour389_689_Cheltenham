THOUGHTS OF THE FOUNDER
(1905)
It is now almost a matter of periodical ex-
pectation that, once in a while the grand juries
will in severe language condemn the irregulari-
ties and delinquencies of the House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys at Cheltenham. The late
grand jury has presented the same old story of
dark cells, inhuman treatment and inability of
the reformatory to reform the boys sent hither.
We are not surprised at any of the revelations
made known. We are, however, surprised that
intelligent and toughtful men of the dominant
races continue to persuade themselves of the
possiblity of accomplishing the very best results
on behalf of those boys as well as in the in-
terest of the community by means of a staff of
officers and teachers of the white race.
Editor and Publisher, 1892-1922.
