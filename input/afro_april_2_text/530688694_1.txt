Good physical condition,
but bad conrtact labor sys-
tem, presence of little 'tots'
under eight and practically
nothing being done to train
the inmates for useful lives,
was the reaction of several
ministers in a group which
visited the House of Refor-
mation at Cheltenham Mon-
day.
The delegation of ministers, head-
ed by the Rev. R. A. Green, will sub-
mit a detailed report of their visit
to the regular session of the preach-
ers meeting next Monday.
Convict Labor
One of the worst features of the
system, declared the Rev. Mr. Green.
is the contract labor wherein the
boys are farmed out to work for out-
siders and during which time they
get no training as should be given
in an institution of this kind. But
the most surprising thing we met
was the presence of mere "tots" un-
der eight. who had been sent there.
The chairman also stated that
while they found the physical cons
ditions excellent, the educational sys-
tem was practically nihil. "I cannot
see." he said. "how any one could
expect the lastitution to help the
hous sent there in so far as it is
designed to train them for useful-
ness."
Lacks Funds
The Rev. W. A. Manokoo stated
that while he did not care to give
out any detailed statement before
the committee set forth its findings
he thought the institution needed
funds with which to inaugurate a
better administration. Those there
at present are courteous and seem-
ingly conscientious, but they lack
funds with which to provide proper
training for the boys.
The whole delegation got a fav
orable impression from the care of
the plant itself. he said. but they
found little being done for the boys
in the way of training.
Many Away At Work
Commenting on conditions there
the Rev. C. H. Stepteau said:
"Upon going to the institution I
found that many of the older boy's
were off at work and I could not
learn some things in detail.
"The favorable thing that I notic-
ed was that the beds were clean and
well made up and the dormitories in
a tidy state.
"The superintendent was courteous
and expressed a desire that more
visitors should come down to see the!
exact state of affairs so that their
criticism could be made from per-
sonal knowledge.
"My adverse criticism would be
that there are small boys there
around the ages of seven, ten and
twelve. Several said they were there
because they were regarded as bad
and there was no other place to send
them.
"Many of them do not know how
long they are to stay there. Some
will be kept until they are 21.
"Some of the larger boys who are
hired out earn money for themselves!
by working overtime. One boy told
me that he earned $11 last week. He
was about 19 years old.
"Considered as a whole. the criti-
cism that I have is of small matters."
Recommendations
The committee will make definite
recommendations. said the Rev. Mr.n
Green, and will make some further co
Investigation as to the presence of
the very young boys they found at
the institution.
