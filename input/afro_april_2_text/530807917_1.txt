Probation Officer Points Out

CASES ARE CITED
Small Boy Commited to Chel-
tenham for Lack of Home
The crying need for a home for
feeble minded boys was shown again
this week, when two small boys, suf-
fering from low mentality, appeared
in juvenile court and one was cont
mitted to Cheltenham for lack of a
better institution.
:
Joseph Thomas, who was brought
into juvenile court by his parents, was
committed to the reform school after
he had run from home for the eighth
time. According to juvenile court
records, the boy was first picked up
on the street, on May 1, 1928, again
on October 10, December 5 and De-
cember 12, 1928. On February 8, 1929,
he again ran away from home, was
sent back, but disappeared again on
February 21, 1929. On this occasion
he was committed to the Cheltenham
School for Boys where he stayed but
a little while and ran away from it.
He was again picked up on January
23. 1930, and again on May 3.
According to the boy's parents, he
was struck by a truck when a child,
and since that time has shown him-
self to be of a feeble mind, and his
greatest fault is running away from
home, although his parents claim
that he gets the best of treatment.
Mr. George Ralph, probation offi-
cer, declares that it is not to the lik-
ing of the juvenile court to commit
boys of this type to Cheltenham, as
there are no facilities there for their
Improvement. City organizations, he
declared should urge the establish-
ment of a home for the feeble mind-
