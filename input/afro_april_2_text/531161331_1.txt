CHELTENHAM TO GET BUILDING
FOLLOWING $15,000 BLAZE
BALTIMORE
According to the Rev. Frederick Douglas, a mem-
ber of the executive board of the Cheltenham School
for Boys, a new building will replace the institution's
$15,000 hospital building which was destroyed by
flames on Sunday.
At the meeting of the committee early in January,
plans and specifications will be taken under advise-
ment. Plans are expected to be drawn up by State
building engineers.
Others on the executive committee are Joseph
Branford, Howard Claggett, John McCormick, and
Willard W. Allen.
A defective flue is said to have caused the fire
at the institution.
