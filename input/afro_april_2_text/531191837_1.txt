Nice Indicates to

Faces Change, Too
BALTIMORE-A colored
personnel and ultimate col-
ored board of directors were
indicated when a delegation
of citizens led by Mrs. As-
bury Smith, chairman of the
Citizens' Committee for Col-
ored Boys, called on Gover-
nor Harry W. Nice to urge
immediate action in adjust-
ing affairs at the Chelten-
ham School on Monday.
So certain was the delegation
that immediate board changes
are inevitable, that it handed the
governor a list of possible ap-
pointments.
Backs Up Executive
In presenting the plea of the
delegation, Mrs. Smith stated that
in view of the governor's proven
interest in action at Cheltenham,
the committee had come only to
indicate a desire to back him up
in the courageous attitude he had
taken.
Calling attention to possi-
ble political pressure on one
side, the group wanted to as-
sure him that he had the
support of the community in
his determination to wipe out
the disgraceful conditions
which existed for such a long
perlod at the reform school.
Adding to what Mrs. Smith
said, Sidney Hollander, a mem-
ber of the State Welfare Board,
reviewed the long fight which
those interested in the institution
had made. Turning to Governor
Nice, he said: "Until you were
elected governor, we were not
able to get to first base in the
matter."
Favor Colored Workers
Both Mrs. Smith and Mr. Hol-
lander advocated acquiring well-
trained colored workers or, some
white workers who understand
what running such an institution
means.
When a survey was made some
time ago, Mr. Hollander said,
"Not one employee at Chelten-
ham was qualified to do the joo
he was expected to do there.
"Now that the institution has
been taken over by the State, the
next job is to get a proper per-
sonnel and the kind of board of
management that will back up a
trained staff and proper pro-
gram."
Wants All Colored
Governor Nice told the dele-
gation that he was thoroughly
committed to a policy of a whol-
ly colored board as well as whol-
ly colored staff at the institution.
