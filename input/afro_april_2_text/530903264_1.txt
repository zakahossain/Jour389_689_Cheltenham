BIG BROTHERS SPENT
$422 FOR NEEDY
Organization Makes Annual
Distribution of Baskets
Christmas Week.

MANY CONTRIBUTIONS
Officials Give Detailed Re-
port of Year's Work.
The Big Brothers organization of
Baltimore distributed $422.20 in ma-
terial to needy families last year, ac-
cording to a report made public by
its officials, this week.
This organization, which for a
number of years was headed by the
late Wm. C. McCard, has become a
fixture in the holiday life of the city.
Their report follows:
In accordance with the custom of our
late president, William C. McCard, who was
so vitally interested in the orphans and
children in the various homes of our city.
we are glad to report to the public that the
response to our appeal this year (1929) for
the above was most generous indeed.
We were able to give the following-235
pounds of the finest turkey, 8 bushels of
white potatoes, 2½ busheis of sweet pota-
toes, 100 pounds of hominy, 22 quarts of
cranberries. 60 pounds of sugar, 50 large
loaves of bread. 44 large mince pies, 325
large apples 325 large oranges and 140
pounds of candy, to six institutions--the
Maryland Home for Friendless Colored Chil-
dren, the St. Mary's Home for Little Boys,
St. Catherine's Home for Little Girls, St.
Luke's Home for Orphans, Industrial Heme
for Orphans and the Apostolic Home for
Neglected Children. Besides we sent $25 to
the St. Frances Home, $25 to the St. Eliza-
beth's Home, and $25 to the Mission Help-
ers-all large homes for children to help
with their Christmas dinner.
To the little boys at Cheltenham, we sent
100 pounds of candy, to the girls at Mel-
vale, 50 pounds of candy, to the little blind
children at Overlea. 50 pounds of candy,
and to the sick children at Provident Hos-
pital, a large basket of oranges and apples.
Finally we sent 1½ tons of coal to the
St. Luke's orphanage and 11½ tons of coal
to the Apostolic orphanage.
Our grateful thanks are due and are giv-
en to the following contributors:
The Emerson Hotel Waiters. Mr. William
White, $25: the AFRO-AMERICAN em-
ployees, $16.50; Mrs. Grace K. McCard, $10.
We are very glad to have Mrs. McCard
as one of our active members.
