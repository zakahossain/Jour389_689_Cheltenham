8 Cheltenham
Boys Get 3 to 9
Months, 1 Freed
UPPER MARLBORO, Md.
One of the nine inmates of the
Cheltenham School for Boys, who
were tried here on Friday before
Magistrate Walter Green on
charges of riotous assembly, was
freed in the custody of his par-
ents, while the others received
sentences of from three to nine
months in the Maryland house of
correction.
At their first trial before him
on December 5, Magistrate Green
found the boys not guilty on the
original charges of assault and at-
tempting to escape and held them
in jail until Friday when the
charge was changed to riotous as-
sembly so that their conviction
and a light sentence might be pos-
sible.

The freed youth is Jesse Wells,
16, of 434 Orchard Street, Bal-
timore, who was given a sus-
pended sentence of six months in
the house of correction and placed
on probation for a year in the
custody of his mother, Mrs.

He was represented by Ben-

of the accused youths with coun-

This disposition of the case was

acting superintendent, and Jo-
