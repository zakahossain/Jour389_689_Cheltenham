Nice Renews
Pledges Made
in Platform
The pledge that he plans to car-
ry out all phases of the platform
of the Republican was renewed
Wednesday by Harry W. Nice, gov-
ernor-elect of Maryland, in a state-
ment to the AFRO-AMERICAN.
Mr. Nice refused to make a com-
ment that would refer to any race
or individual, but he stated that
his chief concern is to devise a
program of relief that will prevent
suffering in the state.
If Mr. Nice carries out the plat-
form, as he has promised, how-
ever, it will automatically mean
that Morgan College will be taken
over by the state and made into
a university.
The reform school for boys at
Cheltenham, Md., will also be in-
vestigated and manned with a
colored staff, according to pre-elec-
tion promises of the Republican
party.
Voting Act to Go
An act known as the Declara-
tion of Intentions Act, which pre-
vents many persons from exercis-
ing their right to vote, and which
the Republican party officials say
hits colored voters hardest, will
possibly be repealed.
The phase of the platform re-
lating to this is:
"We advocate the complete re-
peal of the Declaration of Inten-
tions Act and repeal of the Affi-
davit of Removal Act.
"These pernicious and un-Ameri-
can laws have prevented thousands
of citizens from voting which im-
pose restrictions on the free exer-
cise of suffrage.
"They not only hamper and re-
strict, but in thousands of cases
absolutely prevent voting by per-
sons otherwise entitled."
Minority Group Recognized
Mr. Nice expects to have mi-
nority representation on all boards
and commissions which he shall
appoint during his administration.
At one time Mr. Nice publicly
declared that he was against the
appointment of colored police, be-
cause he did not want to do any-
thing that would arouse racial an-
tagonism in Maryland.
It was rumored that this state-
ment was an effort on the then
candidate's party to curry favor
with the Eastern Shore voters.
Shoreman Wants Police
