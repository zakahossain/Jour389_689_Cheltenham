Maryland Reform School Boys Rioted against Alleged Brutal Guards Until They Fled
Here is Cheltenham din-
ing hall, restored to order
after the inmates rioted,
hurling tin cups, tin plates
and other utensils at guards
who handcuffed a boy Fri-
day night of last week. The
guards fled with minor in-
juries. One guard had his
forehead split. Windows
are used for dense growing
ferns which shut out one-
third of the light in this room.
Ferns are more important
than the children.
A group of boys on the porch of the main building
at Cheltenham, Md., Reform School in Prince Georges
County, which is run by private interests. Despite snow,
ice and freezing weather, these boys are clad only in cot-
ton overalls and cotton underwear. Some are hired out
to white farmers. Their wages go to the school and the
boys are given 50 cents a week. Christmas, some of these
boys, barefooted, were sent to the woods to cut logs.
The institution has been condemned by every grand
jury for 15 years. Welfare surveys recommended the in-
stitution be taken over by the State, but the private board
of directors refused the offer of Governor Nice to take it
over, preferring to hold on to it because it receives over
$100,000 appropriations from the city and State annually
and provides political patronage. (See picture on front
page.)
