Held for Stealing
15 Pounds of Beef
Suspected of stealing a round of
beef from a stall in Lexington Mar-
ket, a 60-year-old man was com-
mitted for the action of the grand
jury, Saturday.
Arraigned before Magistrate Bauer
in Western police court, the man,
Charles Colbert, 60, of 2420 Stock-
ton Street, pleaded not guilty of
the charge.
The operator of the stall, Frank
Knell, reported to Western district
police that the beef had been stol-
en from his stall.
Colbert was arrested on suspi-
cion, and later charged with the
theft of the fifteen pounds of meat
valued at $5.
The lock on the stall had been
broken during the night..
Bail for the accused was set at
$500 and his committment ordered
after he was unable to arrange bail.

POLICE BOARD
LETS COLORED
TAKE EXAM
Examining Board Breaks
Prejudice Bar in Giving
Application Blanks for
Tests.
In conjunction with the recom-
mendation of the recent grand
jury that colored men and women
be appointed to the police force of
Baltimore members of the Board of
Police Examiners expressed willing-
ness Wednesday to accept the appli-
cations of all qualified persons who
wanted to join the force.
The police examiners are prepar-
ing to hold an examination for
headquarters clerks, station house
clerks and signal and telephone
operators. The qualifications neces-
sary for a person to receive an ap-
plication blank is that the appli-
cant be twenty-five years of age, a
citizen of the State of Maryland,
and a registered voter.
Applicants must have a high
school education, never have been
arrested, indicted or convicted of
any violation of the law.
The requirements for the position
of clerk requires that the applicant
be able to take short hand accu-
rately and operate a typewriter
speedily. The first colored to quali-
fy for an application to take the
examination was Levi H. Jolley, a
reporter on the AFRO-AMERICAN,
who received blank No. 854.
