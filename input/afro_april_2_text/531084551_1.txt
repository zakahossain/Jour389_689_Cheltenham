Judging Religions.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
If our religions were to be judged
by each other. there would be no
religions right: if we judged our
own, there would be no religions
wrong.
We differ, but the divine object
brings the human family into one
accord, God is Supreme. God is
all.
(Mrs.) GERTRUDE WILLIAMS
228 Fourth Street,
Lackawanna, N.Y.
Replace Cheltenham.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
After reading of the treatment
afforded our boys at Cheltenham.
I have been wondering whether
it would be a good plan for us to
get together and do something our-
selves to alleviate the situation.
Why can't we get some of our
lawyers, doctors and business men
to purchase a building and take
care of these boys? Our Governor
is a good, just man, and I am sure
he will help.
Baltimore, Md.

Students in Theological
Seminary Dorms
To the Editor of the AFRO:
In a recent report of the Middle
Atlantic Seminary Conference car-
ried in the AFRO this statement
was made: "Students from General
Theological Seminary would take
no part in the discussion, although
colored students there are advised
not to live in the dormitories."
I, and three other colored stu-
dents, live at the seminary. When
I applied for admittance there was
no mention made of my staying
anywhere else. In fact, when I
asked about commuting from my
home I was advised against it be-
cause the distractions involved
would be harmful to my life here
as a student preparing for priest-
hood.
I might also say that my life
here has been most satisfying and
inspiringly pleasant. We all have
the feeling that we are preparing
for the same work in the Catholic
Church, embracing people of many
races and countries, which causes
the petty feeling and prejudices.
that you imply, to sink into the
background.
CHARLES E. HARRISON.
175 Ninth Ave.. New York City
