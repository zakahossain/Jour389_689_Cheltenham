Hearing Set in
Fatal Gas Blast
BALTIMORE. The pre-
liminary hearing for John Lemon,
27, of 515 Archer Street, held
without bail at Southwestern po-
lice station in connection with the
death of James Walker of 512 W.
West Street, has been set for
August 16.
Mr. Walker died on July 19 as
the result of burns received from
a gasoline explosion in Lemon's!
cellar the night before.
According to Cain Ballard, 24,
of 904 S. Sharp Street, he and
Mr. Walker were visiting Lemon
when the three went to the cel-
lar to get coal oil for a lamp
from a drum there.
Lamp Ignites Oil
Unable to siphon the oil from
the drum, Ballard said Walker
picked it up and began to pour
the contents into a five-gallon can
when, suddenly, the lamp flared
up, igniting the coal oil and their
clothing.
Police report that Ballard took
Walker and Lemon to the hos
pital in the latter's car, where
Mr. Walker died the next morning,
ninety per cent of his body char-
red by the fire.
The other two men suffered
burns of the arms and legs, but
were later released from the hos-
pital.
