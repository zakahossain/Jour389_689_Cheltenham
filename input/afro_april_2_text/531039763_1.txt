Special to the AFRO-AMERICAN
BOSTON Charged with hav-
ing a love affair with a 15-year-old
girl, who is expected to become a
mother in November, the Rev.
Wilbur W. Henry, 22-year-old
pastor of the Holy Trinity Bap-
tist Church, and a former mem-
ber of the famed Henry Brothers'
evangelist team, pleaded not
guilty, when arraigned before
Judge Timothy Ahearn in the
Roxbury Court, Monday.
His case was continued until
the 19th. The former evangelist
was released in $2,000 bail.
According to the police, a war-
rant was issued for the arrest of
the handsome "boy-pastor" when
a complaint was made by the
mother of the allegedly abused
girl. The abuse is said to have
occurred on February 19.
Followers Fill Court
The large corridors of the Rox-
bury court-house was filled with
loyal members of the accused pas-
tor's flock. Female and male
parishioners surrounded the deb-
onair Rev. Mr. Henry, expressed
their confidence in him, and pre-
dicted his acquittal.
The youthful former evangelist,
with tears rolling down his eyes,
made the following statement for
the AFRO:
"I am a victim of a frame-up.
This is the second attempt made
by my enemies to ruin my suc-
cessful, youthful ministerial ca-
reer. It is all the result of care-
ful plotting of my enemies, who
are jealous of my success at the
Holy Trinity Baptist Church."
"Scarcely Knew Girl"
Trembling with uncontrollable
emotion, the "boy-pastor" declar-
ed that he scarcely knew the
girl, and only remembers her as
a recent addition to his growing
number of parishioners.
In the tone of voice which made
him famous as an evangelist, the
Rev. Mr. Henry calmly added:
"Sometime ago, an unidentified
informant told me that my
enemies were out to get my scalp,
and shake me down. This at-
tempted shake-down was to be
made because it is the consensus
of opinion that my family is
wealthy, which is not true. But
I will fight this case to the highest
courts of the land."
Prayers Requested
Making a request to the "kindly
AFRO." the former evangelist
said he wanted, "his friends
throughout the country to pray
for him that he will be able to
conquer the devil, who is making
a diabolical fight to overthrow
him and destroy his youthful
ministerial career."
"Pray for me," he requested,
"that I might be able to stand
firm on solid rock with Jesus
Christ."
The arrest of the Rev. Mr.
Henry created a furore in church
circles here. Several months ago
he ran afoul of the law when he
was arrested by Officer Parker A.
Coleman of the Roxbury Cross-
ing station on a drunkenness
charge, but his case was subse-
quently placed on file.
According to the police, the
youthful pastor put up a stiff bat-
tle with Officer Coleman and po-
lice clubs were brought into ac-
tion to subdue him. He was char-
acterized by the police as
"fighting parson."
