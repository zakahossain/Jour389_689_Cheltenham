Hall, Echols Missing as Va. State
Preps for Opener Against Bluefield
PETERSBURG, Va.
"Things
are looking brighter," was all that
big Harry Jefferson, coach at Vir-
ginia State, said this week as he
surveyed the nicest crop of fresh-
men huskies that has been seen
in the Trojan camp during his six
years as head football mentor,
during which he has produced two
championship teams.
Last year, State won six games,
tied one, and lost none to win
the CIAA Conference top honors.
It was Bluefield who played State
to a 0-0 tie. On October 7, State
opens against an unchanged Blue-
field team, which last year was
manned by sophomores for the
most part.
Hall, Echols, Missing
The grim spectres of gradua-
tion, poor scholarship, and finan-
cial difficulties have reared their
ugly heads to cause havoc in the
Trojan camp this season. First se-
vere blow came with the an-
nouncement that Jumping Joe
Echols would not return to har-
rass the opposition. Most recent
set-back is the rumor
not yet
confirmed
that Joe Hall, speed
merchant
from
Philadelphia,
would be among the missing.
Of the 44 men reporting, an un-
lucking 13 were veterans, while
31 were freshmen Although
there undoubtedly will be a steady
stream of men reporting through-
out the week, it is doubtful that
many of these will be veterans.
Coach Jefferson has already pre-
dicted that the Trojan machine
of 1939 will be the greenest that
State has put on the playing field
in many a year.
With few, if any, of the return-
ing linemen being anything to
write home about, there will be
a mad scramble for regular line
berths. Already the natural abili-
ty of several of the newcomers
has attracted the critical eyes of
the coaching staff.
Three freshmen look particular-
ly good in the light drills-Charles
Lee, Kenneth Whitlock and Ted
McCain. Lee is a Charlottesville
boy, who is looked upon as one
of the best prospects of the new
timber, while Whitlock, a Penn-
sylvania boy, was a three-letter
man in football, basketball and
track while in high school. Both
are better than average kickers.
McCain, a Boston boy, has all that
a good passer needs.
To Stress Passes
With Coach Jefferson planning
to develop an overhead game to
offset a shoddy line, passers and
kickers will bask in the limelight
as keymen this season. It will be
Dicky Burr, diminutive quarter-
back, who looks like All-Ameri-|
can timber this year, who will
play the stellar role of kicker par
excellence. Dicky's toe will bear
the major share of the burden if
the Trojans kick their way to
another CIAA championship.
New Men Shine
Other newcomers who looked
good in the first drill of the sea-
son were: John Jackson, who
comes from Youngstown, Ohio,
and will be sweating for a back-
field berth; James Burton, who
starred at end for Braddock High
in Pennsylvania; Shirley Jordan,
a Norfolk boy who comes well-
recommended from Booker T.
Washington High School; Coppin
Cartwright, from Phindelphia's
Cheltenham High School; and
Earl Holmes, a Hampton boy, who
has hopes of landing a backfield
post,
