A Summary of the Week’s News

The State’s first triple hanging
in fourteen years occurred Fri-
day when Frank (Zipp) Williams,
25; Freeman Holton, 22, and Wil-
bur Jackson, 22, were executed
for the holdup-slaying in August,
1941, of Louis Pertnoy, white,
manager of an East Baltimore
theatre.

The season’s first two swim-
ming victims were Nathaniel
Richardson, 18, of 25 N. Eden
Street, and William Plummer, Jr.,
21, of 2308 Guilford Avenue, who
drowned in the harbor, Thurs-
day.

A free-for-all battle among 20
of 200 native Bahamans at the
FSA farm labor camp at Hebron,
Md., near Salisbury, resulted in
the arrest of eight. One couple,
James Armstrong, said to have
agitated the fight over food and
wages, and his wife, Susan, are
being held for deportation.

Drop Charges Against Clarke

Insubordination charges against
Daniel Clarke, educational super-
visor at Cheltenham School for
Boys, resulting in his suspension,
have been withdrawn.

A hearing to determine whether
the late Edward Haywood, sweep-
stakes winner, was of sound mind
when he willed a large portion of
his $60,000 estate to a brother
and nieces instead of to his wife
and child is being held by City
Court.

Seventy-one Morgan State Col-
lege graduates, eight of whom
are in the army, received degrees.

Dr. David A. Robertson, white,
president of Goucher College, was
commencement speaker.

Eleven graduates of Provident
Hospital’s nursing school, heard
Dr. R. O’Hara Lanier, acting pres-
ident of Hampton Institute, at
commencement exercises.

The annual recital series by pi-
ano and vocal pupils of Mrs. Adah
K. Jenkins opened Sunday at
Enon Baptist Church.

Church News

Providence Baptist Church, the
Rev. E. W. White, pastor, burned
its $40,000 mortgage on Sun-
day.

Forty-five persons, including a
family of five and four married
couples, were confirmed at St.
James PE Church by Bishop
Noble C. Powell.

Sports Activities

The School for the Handicap-
ped captured the city-wide track
and field championship for oc-
cupational schools at Druid Hill
Park. William M. Alexander
School No. 112 was second.

In two baseball games against
the Homestead Grays, the Balti-
more Elite Giants won 11 to 8
and 2 to 0.

Buddy Walker of Columbia,
Ohio, won a decision over Charley
Eagle of Waterbury, Conn., in
twelve rounds at the Coliseum.
