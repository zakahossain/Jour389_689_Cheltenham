Incensed over the new system of
assigning sanitary facilities on a
segregated basis at the Western!
Electric plants, employees threat-
ened to quit their jobs.
The threats highlighted a meet-
ing sponsored bythe Non-Partisan

Colored officers who assisted
Lieutenant Emerson's vice squad
in three week-end raids in which
fifty-one persons were hailed into
Northwestern police station, were:
Officers Joseph Duggins and Den-
nis Mello and Auxiliary Police-
men Charles Brunes and Arthur
Caldwell.
Cheltenham Proposal Hit
The local Urban League has ex-
pressed disapproval of the plan
to renovate the Cheltenham
School for Boys rather than to con-
struct a new institution as au-
thorized in a letter to Gov. Herb-
ert R. O'Conor.
No colored military police have
yet been assigned to the Pennsyl-
vania Avenue area as promised by
Major-General Philip Hayes, com-
manding general of the Third
Service Command.
The annual meeting of the Mary-
land State Conference of NAACP
Branches will be held May 27 at
Union Baptist Church, 1219 Druid
Hill Avenue.
In a tribute to mothers of serv-
ice men, Mrs. Fannie Pinkney of
2123 Madison Avenue was hon-
ored as the ideal mother at the
Gold Street USO.
Whites Like "Negro Soldier"
The film, "The Negro Soldier,"
shown concurrently at two local
white theatres, received favorable
responses, cheers and spasmodic
bursts of handclapping.
The Federal Court decision
which upheld the policy of the
Enoch Pratt Free Library in bar-
ring colored women from its train-
ing classes was appealed on May
10 by the NAACP through its at-
torneys, W. A. C. Hughes and
Charles H. Houston.
