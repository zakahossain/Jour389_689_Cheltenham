A picture resembling the
notorious "hell hole of Shan-
ghai" was painted by Judge
J. S. Waxter of the juvenile
court. in describing conditions
at the girls' reformatory
school at Melvale, in a talk to
the Interdenominational Min-
isterial Alliance of white and
colored ministers at the Cen-
tral Y.M.C.A., Monday.
Speaking on "Delinquent Youth
and the Church," the judge declared
that conditions at this school were,
the worst of any such place in the
entire country.
There are about 100 girls there, ac-
cording to Judge Waxter, and 65 per
cent of them have some form of so-
cial disease. Most of these cases, he
says were contracted in the institu-
tion, statements by the girls showed.
Sex Abnormality
In nearly every case the girls in-
dulge in homosexual practice,s he
said. Each girl who comes to the
institution is initiated into the prac-
tice by older inmates.
He found in one extreme case one
of the girls had been cut by another
with a razor as a result of homo-
sexual practice. Another who was
burned, said she enjoyed it as a sex
experience.
Eat With Hands
At one visit made by Judge Waxter
he found them eating their meal of
liver and gravy with their hands.
This is the practice there, he was
told. There is no school and the one
teacher there admitted that she had
no bool:s with which to teach so that
only six or seven girls get any school-
ing at all.
None of the girls has sufficient
clothing nor enough and proper bed
clothing.
The condition of the toilets was
almost ndescribable, said the jurist,
and at one visit that he made, only
one was in proper working condition.
There were rat holes and tremendous
filth, he says.
Should Fight
Judge Waxter declared earnestly
that a fight should be made against!
these conditions.
In describing conditions at Chel-
tenham, Judge Waxter declared that
they used to be abominable there.
but were now slightly improved. Even
there, he said, they do nothing con-
structive for the inmates.
Untrained Staff
There are only two people on the
staff at Cheltenham who have suffi-
cient training to be there, he said,
and there is no vocational training
nor proper recreation.
Judge Waxter attributed most of
the bad moral condition at these in-
stitutions to lack of proper recrea-
tion. Girls at Melvale all work in a
factory during the day and after eat-!
ing their evening meal go to their
dormtory and to bed. There. in the
absence of proper recreational outlet.
thev degenerate into sordid practices
I to amuse themselves.
Juvenile Court
The local juvenile court is making
an effort to raise the standard of juv-
enile treatment. Social, physical and
psychiatric examinations are being
made af all cases brought in.
Seventy-five per cent of children
from three to four years of age have
retarded mentality. Sixty-five of the
delinkuent cases among girls are sex
cases.
Mostly Protestant
The judge finds that the Protest-
ant churches are not dealing as suc-
cessfully with child life as the Catho-
lics are. Most of the girls who come
in are from Protestant homes. Catho-
lics. he said, get in early training and
plant holy fear into children.
In commenting on the disclosures
of Judge Waxter, the Rev. George F.
Bragg, Jr., declared that when he
was on the board he advocated the
appointment of colored women. The
white women there were not of a
high type, he said.
A committee was appointed to start
reform remedies at both Melvale and
Cheltenham.
Matron Admits Charges
In a statement to the AFRO-
AMERICAN, Tuesday, Mrs. Sarah
Medinger, now in charge of the Re-
form School for Colored Girls at Mel-
vale, admitted that Judge Waxter's
statement was about right when she
went there last January.
"The girls," she said. "have no
knives and forks because they are
considered a menace. They have
plenty of food and use spoons. The
clothing is better now than it was
when I came here. The building,
which is an old one and inadequate,
is kept as clean as possible. Lack
of funds is the chief obstacle.
"Additional lavatories have been
placed but homesexual practices still
continue. While the school facilities
have improved some, there are still
lacking books. Recreation is not
sufficient. We have a victrola and
piano and the girls can sing and
