Nice Names Five to
Reform School Boards
Appointments of five persons to
the boards of two Maryland cor-
rectional Institutions were an-
nounced this week by Governor
Harry W. Nice.
Joseph C. Briscoe, principal of
the vocational school, and the
Rev. Frederick Douglas, pastor of
Cosmopolitan Commur.ity Church,
are named to the board of man-
agement of the Cheltenham Re-
formatory for boys, the other eight
members being white.
On the board of the Maryland
Training School for Girls at
Glenburnie, Governor Nice ap-
pointed Miss Adeline Green and
Mrs. Howard E. Young, both of
Baltimore, and Charles Oliver of
Annapolis.
The positions carry no salary,
according to Marse Callaway,
Fourth District Republican leader.
The appointments of the mem-
bers of the Governor's Commis-
sion on Higher Education were
also confirmed this week.
