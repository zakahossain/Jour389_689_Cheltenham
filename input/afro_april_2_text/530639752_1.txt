STILL FARMED OUT
Boys are still farmed out to work
outside the institution, he said, and
although the practice was stopped for
a while in 1924, that at the present
time a number of the boys go out on
Monday and return Saturday nite.
Lilly, himself, has been working for
sometime at Clinton, Md., with a
farmer, who up until his discharge
gave him fifty cents every week for
spending money. This was all the
compensation he himself got. Not a
cent was given him, he said when he
left Cheltenham:
