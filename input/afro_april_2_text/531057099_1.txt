No Operation Yet
on Cheltenham
Boy Shot Last Week
WASHINGTON-Aubrey Brun-
son, 21-year-old Baltimore boy,
who was shot by a guard at the
House of Reformation, in Chel-
tenham, Md., last week, is still in
Freedmen's Hospital recovering
from the bullet wound in the up-
per abdomen.
According to Brunson's state-
ment to an AFRO reporter this
week, no operation has been per-
formed yet to remove the bullet.
Attending physicians would not
give out any information con-
cerning the treatment that had
been given.
Brunson, appearing to be weak,
said that he frequently suffered
sharp pains in his abdomen and
side. He is being attended by
Dr. L. Walberg, of the hospital
staff.
Issue was taken with reports
carried in last week's issue of
the AFRO that all whipping was
done in the superintendent's of-
fice. Brunson declared that any
of the guards administered cor-
poral punishment whenever and
wherever they wanted to, and
that the superintendent was not
necessarily around when the pun-
ishment was being administered.
Punishments such as bending
over and touching the toes for
seven hours a day for a week;
solitary confinement; standing on
one foot for long hours at a time,
were described by Brunson as
"only a few of the many hor-
rible punishments we get."
Since being in Freedmen's Hos-
pital, Brunson has been visited
by his mother and sister and
brother. No officials from the re-
formatory are known to have vis-
ited him since he was brought
here last week under heavy guard.
