68-Year-Old Descendant
of Tecumseh Passes
CHARLOTTE, N.C.-(ANI)-
Death claimed John Watt, 68-
year-old three-quarter Indian
blood, here early Friday a his
home, where he had lived with
his aged wife, Mrs. Ellen Watt,
for nearly thirty years.
He was one of the few remain-
'ng 'bloods" left whose paternal
ancestry descended from the line
of Tecumseh, famed Indian
chieftian. The family had one
daughter who died six years ago.

HELPS DAD LAY
CORNERSTONE
YOUNGSTOWN, O.-The corner-
stone of the new $200,000 "Y" branch
building was laid here last Sunday,
December 28, at 4 o'clock by Council-
man W. S. Vaughn and Simeon S.
Booker, Jr. The address was de-
livered by U.S. Congressman John
G. Cooper, white, with music by the
"Y" branch boys' band of twenty-one
pieces.
The building was made possible
through the great expansion cam-
paign of 1929 in which the Y.M.C.A.
raised $1,176,000 the opening night,
closing the campaign before it of-
ficially opened.
The growth of this branch has been
marvelous. From nothing in 1926 to
an organization of six hundred mem-
bers, men and boys divided into clubs
and welfare groups, covering every
section of the city and representing
every type of our race life. Four
hundred or more people shivered in
the cold to witness this epoch-mak-
ing feature.
Secretary S. S. Booker was for-
merly head of the Baltimore
Y.M.C.A.
Crashing Autos May
Prove Fatal for Woman
Victim Unconscious Three Days
while Husband's Mangled Hand
is Amputated
COKESBURY, Md. Three per-
sons were injured severely and one,
Mrs. John Forrester, is expected to
die from injuries received when their
auto was crashed into by a car con-
taining white occupants, Saturday
night, on the Swan Road as the trio
was en route to Aberdeen.
Mrs. Forrester suffered a concus-
sion of the brain, internal injuries
and a broken jaw bone. She had
not regained consciousness on Tues-
day at the Harve de Grace Hospital.
The right hand of her husband, John
Forrester, was so badly mangled
from glass that it had to be am-
putated. David E. Smith, the third
member of the party, received a dis-
located shoulder.
The white persons occupying the
second car were also taken to the
hospital, but neither their identity
nor the extent of their injuries was
known at press time.
The machine which collided with
the Forresters is said to have been
swerving to miss a third machine
with Pennsylvania licenses, coming
out of a side road, when the acci-
dent occurred. The machine causing
the collision did not stop,
Both autos concerned in the mis-
hap were demolished.

formatory, was held for action of
the grand jury, charged with enter-

are being held as a result of a gun
battle staged near Huntsville, Md.,
on Christmas Day, when Thomas
Washington and Jesse Allen were
slightly wounded.

who is alleged to have forced off the
road a machine driven by Harvey
Sharrow, white, of 927 G Street
