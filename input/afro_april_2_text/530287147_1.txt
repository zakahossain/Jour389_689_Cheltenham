INDUSTRIAL
FAIR CLOSES.
One Of The Most Successful In
The History Of The State
Thousands of People Crowd
To Examine The Various Ex-
hibits.

The State Industrial Fair, under
the auspices of the Most Worshipful
United Grand Lodge, F. & A. Ma-
sons, of Maryland, which opened on
Monday, November 25th, in the
Centre Market Hall, closed on Mon-
day night, when over 2500 people
crowded every part of the spacious
hall. The came in singles, in dou-
bles, in triples and in crowds, until
the ticket sellers were worn out
selling tickets and had to be relieved
by others. It was a record breaking
crowd.

There were hundreds of exhibits,
many of them of considerable merit,
on exhibition. Certainly the Afro-
Americans have no reason to be
ashamed of the splendid exhibition
they have made at this the first in-
dustrial fair held in this State for
many years. And it is reasonable
to suppose that when the next fair
shall be held, with proper manage-
ment, the exhibit that can be made
will surprise even the colored peo-
ple themselves.
