Yuletide Made
Happy for Boys
at Cheltenham
-
CHELTENHAM, Md. The
holiday season was a happy. one
for the boys at the Cheltenham
School because many Baltimore
men and women, at the suggestion
of the school's auxiliary, made
contributions of money
and
gifts.
The auxiliary of the school in-
cludes Mrs. Bertha Proctor, Miss
Ida Cummings, Miss Vivian
Young, Mrs. Rose D. Hill, Mrs.
Fannie Tibbs, Policewoman Violet
Hill Whyte and Edward S.
Lewis.
Miss Cummings furnished fifty-
two bags of candy and peanuts
for the boys and collected $51
which was turned over to the
school for the purchase of gifts
for those boys who have no fam-
ilies.
Sorority Gives Money
Miss Young, representing the
Pi Lambda Psi honorary society
of Morgan State College, made a
donation of $35 with which games
and toys for smaller boys were
purchased.
Policewoman Whyte secured a
contribution of nine dozen fleece-
lined gloves from Captain Joseph
Wallace and Lieutenant Edward
Coughlin of the Northwestern
police district, and other mem-
bers of the committee made per-
sonal donations with which toys.
games, clothing and gifts were
purchased.
On Christmas Day, each of the
boys were treated to a turkey
dinner given by the school and
also received fruits and candy.
