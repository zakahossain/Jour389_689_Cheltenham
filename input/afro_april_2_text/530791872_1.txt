HE IS IN NEW YORK
Mrs. Flossie Monroe Obtains
Decree in Local Court.
Testifying that her husband, Albert
W. Monroe, Brooklyn, deserted her
while she lay ill in bed, for the love
of another woman, Mrs. Flossie M.
Monroe, 2411 Etting Street, was
granted an absolute divorce in the
circuit cour', Friday.
In her statement to the examiner,
Mrs. Monroe declared that she was
married in Brooklyn, N.Y., on De-
cember 18, 1926. During the period
they lived together, her husband
treated her with exceeding cruelty,
and would stay away from home for
several days at a time.
She was taken ill on February 26,
1927, and on learning of her illness
he stayed away five days. When he
returned on March 1, he took his
clothing, declaring that he was in
love with another woman.
Relatives who testified for Mrs.
Monroe corroborated her statements.
There were no children. Mrs. Monroe
was granted the resumption of her
maiden name, Flossie M. DeLoach.
