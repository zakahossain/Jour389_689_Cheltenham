GRAND JURY RE-VISITS REFORM SCHOOL
BODY WOULD ASK
CHANGES AT
CHELTENHAM
Grand Jury Committee
Formulates Report
Following Visit.
STATE CONTROL'
Favor Colored Teachers
and Other Changes.
Another grand jury report
will recommend sweeping
changes at the House of Re-
formation for Colored Boys at
Cheltenham, it was indicated
in a committee hearing of
that body Tuesday evening in
the grand jury room.
The conference, into which was
called a number of citizens to consid-
er the matter, was headed by Grand
Juryman C. M. Falconer, who headed
the special committee which visited
the institution on an inspection trip
last Wednesday.
Some Improvements
While a number of improvements
were cited by the chairman, including
the abolishment of the old practice
of farming out boys for work, and
the achievement of better physical
conditions, it was stated that under
the present management it was fail-
ing to meet the problems of a reform
school and that sweeping changes
were needed.
The chief criticism brought out at
the meeting was to the effect that
teaching and industrial activities
there were not preparing the boys
for citizenship. Statistics show that a
large percentage of the boys leaving
Cheltenham keep straight on to jail
and the penitentiary under the pres-
ent regime. It was also brought out
that this is the result with boys taken
there, not as delinquents, but be-
cause their families break up or die
and they become wards of the com-
munity.
Politics was also blamed for failure
to get a change of management at
the institution.
Race Teachers
The discussion indicated that the
Grand Jury report would recommend
among other things, race teachers,
state control of the institution, or
the shutting off of municipal funds
until conditions are remedied.
The Grand Jury Cheltenham com-
mittee comprises: C. M. Falconer,
chairman; James E. Preston, Jr.,
Herbert A. Gillespie, William H.
Crane. Frederick P. Steiff (all white),
and W. Emmett Coleman. Called into
the conference were Dr. Howard
Young, Miss Georgianna Fields. R.
Maurice Moss, Probation Officer
George Ralph and Willian. N. Jones.
