BOYS CLUBBED
IN CHELTENHAM
SAY INMATES
Former Inmates Writes Afro-
American Of Barbarities
Practiced At Reform School
PRISON METHOD USED
Solitary Cells, Bread And
Water Diet, Made Skeletons
Of Boys, Is Charge
That the worst form of crulety and
shameful clubbinig of boys with clubs
were practiced at the House of Re-
formation at Cheltenham, is the
charge made by William M. Creasy,
for more than three years an inmate
of the institution, to this paper this
week.
Creasy, who volunteered this infor-
Is mation, offers to come here as a wit-
ness and testify as to detall happen-
dings which took place while he was
there. His letter follows:
Editor AFRO-AMERICAN:
I was reading last week's AFRO,
and I noticed your Cheltenham ar-
ticle.
I was an inmate of the House of
Reformation in 1912, and I can
frankly say that every word in your
article is true. The only thing you
have not found out half that goes
on on the inside of the place.
Bread and tea is certainly the
chief diet, and not much of that.
Two slices of bread and half a cup
of tea to a person at breakfast and
supper. At dinner the same bread
and tea with a bowl of worm broth
which they called soup.
Work Without Food
When the officers are angry at
something they cut the boys out of
three and four meals hand running
and make them work just the same.
They have big straps there which
they beat the boys with, and they
have handles 2½ feet long and the
Ilmber part which boys are hit with
is just a little longer.
The farm overseer is a big colored
man weighing 225 or 250 pounds, and
he is the chief beater. Mr. J. B.
Pyles, the superintendent, used to
give his overseer, whose name is
Harry Washington, a quart of whis-
key and the strap, and sometimes
they would go to all five of the
schools and beat all the boys un-
mercifully.
Dies After Blow
Whispering or talking is strictly
against the rules, except to an of-
ficer or guard. While I was there
one boy, John Baker, received a
blow over the head with a window
stick, and he died a few weeks after
getting the blow. E. D. Furgang,
the teacher at that time of No. 4
school, was the man who delivered
the blow.
I was beaten nearly to death be-
cause I sobbed too loud when I re-
ceived a letter that my mother was..
dead. This was about the third
month that I was there.
Prison Methods Used
When a boy would run away he
was confined in a small cell, 8 x 3,
from 3 to 6 months. His diet was
two thin clices of bread, two cups
of water a day, the first thirty days
he was confined. The superinten-
dent would bring Washington around
every day, and the boy who was in
confinement would receive a severe
beating every day for the first thirty
days. By this time these boys would
look like walking skeletons.
Two School Days In Three Years
I was at Cheltenham 3 years, 5
months, and a few days, and I had '
school two afternoons during this
time.
They would hire the boys out with
mean people who hated colored peo-
ple and the boys were worked just
3 like slaves: They gave us a fairly
good sized piece of meat each
Thanksgiving, and this the only
time we did get any meat.
I was sent to, Cheltenham from
this town and the men who sent me
are still here. If they are making
an investigation of the House of
Reformation and need a witness who
realy knows the inside of the place
I am the man.
Very truly yours,
WM. M. CREASY.
