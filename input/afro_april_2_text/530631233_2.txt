CHELTENHAM OFFICERS Colonel Hill To
INVITED BY PASTORS
Open Campaign
Robert Roberts, president of the
board of managers of Cheltenham,
and Secretary Janney of the same
institution were urged by a commit-
tee from the A. M. E. Preachers'
Meeting to appear before that body
next Monday at 11 o'clock and ex-
plain the operation of the House
of Reformation for Colored Boys,
located in Prince Georges County.
At a meeting of the ministers
Monday at Bethel Church, refer-
ence was made to the repeated re-
ports of the mismanagement of the
institution and a motion prevailed
to invite the Cheltenham officials
before the pastors so that first hand
information might be obtained.
The committee of pastors delegat-
ed which called at the offices of
Secretary Janney this week was
Dr. C. H. Stepteau, Dr. M. H. Davis,
and Dr. W. H. Thomas.
Letters sent out from the office
of Colonel John Philip Hill announce
that his campaign for the Senate
will open two weeks from today in
the Broadway Market Hall, Broad-
way and Fleet streets.
Speakers announced will be Sen-
ator Joseph I. France, Thomas Par-
ran, Marion A. Humphreys, William
F. Broening and Col. Hill.
Attacking the record of ex-Sena-
tor Weller, Col. Hill said this week,
since Mr. Weller took office he has
been absent or not voting 55.1 per
cent of the quorum calls and yea
and nay votes. He has been absent
more than one half of the time. In
the five years of his incumbency,
Senator Weller has made but two
committee reports. He has not ut-
tered one word to the Senate in ad-
vocacy of a good bill or in opposi-
tion to a bad one.
