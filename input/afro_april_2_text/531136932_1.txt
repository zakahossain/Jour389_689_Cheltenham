The League Has Failed.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
The League of Nations, pre-
dominated by England and France,
has proved to the world its utter
failure as an arbitrative body in
carrying out the program of its
responsibility of safeguarding the
rights of the weaker nations sub-
scribing to its convenant.
As a black man I see its failure
when Italy is allowed to run riot
in Ethiopia in defiance of justice
and Christian Good-will, thus
violating the sacredness of peace.
J. B. HOWARD
Charleston, S.C.

Flowers for Cheltenham
Principal.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
Much has been said and written
about the Cheltenham Reform
School, but few people know of
the monumental work being done
there by Chancellor Williams, the
young principal who took charge
last September. Mr. Williams, a
graduate of Howard University,
has been attacking the various
problems. in spite of all handicaps,
with the dauntless courage of an
experienced administrator.
He reorganized the school system
from top to bottom. He abolished
the cottage system of instruction
and classified the boys upon the
basis of achievement and intelli-
gence tests. He introduced the
Dalton Laboratory Plan of instruc-
tion and the Big Brothers' Hour.
He is publishing this month the
first edition of the Cheltenham
Boys Messenger. Courses in Negro
histo:y have been included in all
upper grades. In this work, Mr.
Williams is being assisted by three
other Howard men: James E. Har-
rison, Jr., Howard P. Banks, and
L. S. Wilson.
HAROLD_N. SLAUGHTER
