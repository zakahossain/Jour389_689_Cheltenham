General improvement of

throughout the State, the es-

The same convention nominated

clerk of the court of appeals:
Equal Opportunity
A reference which leaders inter-
preted as expressing the view of
party leaders on the Negro status in
Maryland Republicanism came when
former Senator Joseph I. France,
closing a general endorsement of all
candidates declared that if the
Maryland Free State meant any
thing it should mean free and equal
opportunities for every citizen, white
ad colored.
Dr. France's speech, in which he
warned the delegates that it would
be best to boost their own candidates
rather than adopt a hammering
campaign against Governor Ritchie,
the Democratic candidate, was in
contrast to practically every other
speaker who rapped the present
governor for his fourth term

The plank which referred to Ne-
gro institutions was as follows:
We are impressed with the marked
advance of the colored race in Mary-
land and elsewhere. In Baltimore this
has been accomplished in considerable
measure by the helpful and sympathetic
attitude of Mayor Broening. We com-
mend the removal of the notorious
"lung block" and the provision of more
adequate school facilities for the col-
ored race in Baltimore city.
We favor the general improvement
of school facillites for colored children
throughout the State.
The present State administration
has failed in its duty to erect an
asylum for the feeble-minded and other
mentally deficient colored children.
The Republican party recognizes the
State's obligation to provide Imme-
diately such an institution.
We further pledge ourselves to re-
organize the management of the Chel-
tenham Institution and the Melvale
School, and to terminate the abuses
which now exist therein.
Varied Opinions
Just what the plank means as to
the main objective of Negro leaders
in equalizing salaries of white and
Negro teachers in the state, varied
in the opinions of several gathered
at the convention Monday. It was
known that a much more definite
plank had been proposed which
would pledge the Republican party
to amend the law and equalize the
minimum pay for public school
teachers in the state.
Friends of Mayor Broening, how-
ever, pointed to the support he has
given the equalization policy in the
Baltimore schools and declare that
should he become governor, he would
without doubt appoint men on the
State Board of Education who would
favor equal educational opportunities
for all citizens, white and colored.
Paul Berman Talks
Paul Berman, white candidate for
State Senate from the Fourth Dis-
trict, told an AFRO representative.
that he was certain a Broening ad-
ministration of state education would
bring the system up to the same
equal condition of the Baltimore
public schools, and that he favored
and would work for this if elected
to the Senate.
Although the prohibition plank
took up a large part of the hour
and one half session of the resolu-
tions committee held after organiza-
tion of the convention, it was also
known that the wording of the Ne-
gro plank came in for serious con-
sideration.
