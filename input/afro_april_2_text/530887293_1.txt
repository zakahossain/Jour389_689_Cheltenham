RITCHIE'S BUDGET
TO LEGISLATURE
Appropriations to State-Aided Insti-
tutions, Bowie, Morgan, Same as 1930
ANNAPOLIS, Md.-Governor Rit-
chie's budget for 1932 and 1933
went to the legislature Tuesday.
Appropriations were in the main,
the same as last year. Previously
the governor approved additions to
Crownsville Hospital for the Insane,
a new school for feebleminded child-
ren and a new Melvale Reformatory
for Cirls.
Provident Hospital was allowed a
$2,500 increase. Other items were:
A.M.E. Home for Aged, $1,500.
| Morgan College was allowed $22,-
000. It had requested $43,500 and the
education commission approved an
$8,000 increase.
| Shelter for Aged and Infirm, $500.
Maryland Home for Friendless
Colored Children, $500.
St. Elizabeth's Home for Colored
Children, $12,000.
St. Mary's Home for Colored Boys,
$1,250, increase of $500; had asked
for $2,000.
St. Peter Claver's Industrial School,
$3,000.
St. Katharine's Home for Little
Colored Girls, $1,250: increase of
$500, had asked for $2,000.
Cheltenham Home of Reformation
for Colored Boys, $20,000; had re-
quested $45,000.
Industrial Home for Colored Girls,
$10,000.
Provident Hospital, $12,000; increase
of $2,500; $17,500 had been request-
ed.
Home of Good Shepherd, $4,000.
Gibbons Institute, $2,000; $5,000
had been requested.
Johns Hopkins received its first
state appropriation. The sum was
$15,000. It had asked for $25,000.
Maryland School for Blind (Col-
ored), $15,000.
State Institutions
| Crownsville State Hospital for the
Insane-$256,200, increase of $57,-
000. New buildings requested. Feeble-
minded School, 100 beds, $280,000;
Dormitory, T.B., 100 beds, $165,000:
addition to Hugh Young Building, 100
beds, $165.000.
Henryton T.B. Sanitarium's whole
budget included in that of Maryland
T.B. Sanitarium, branches.
Bowie State Normal School-$26,-
180, increase of $150; salaries, $28,500,
operating expenses, decrease of $600.
New buildings requested: principal's
home, $10,000; girl's dormitory, 40
rooms, $25,000.
