Ofay Won't Die
for 2 Murders
JACKSON, Miss.---The sentence
of William Clark Mitchell, 54-
year-old white man, scheduled to
die on December 17 for the mur-
der of Lewis Bryant, farmer, and
his son, Coot Bryant, was com-
muted to life imprisonment by
Governor Hugh White last week.
Had Mitchell gone to the gal-
lows, he would have been the
second white man in Mississippt
to die for the murder of a col-
ored man.
Cheltenham Loses
Hospital in Fire
UPPER MARLBORO, Md. A
$15,000 fire which volunteers of
three towns battled in sub-freez.
ing weather with ice-caked hose
lines, destroyed all but the walls
of the two-story hospital at the
Cheltenham School for Boys,
Monday.
Four young patients at the in-
stitution were rescued safely and
removed to other buildings. The
firemen were aided by youthful
inmates in confining the blaze to
the hospital.
72-YEAR-OLD MAN
FACES ASSAULT CHARGE
ASBURY PARK. N.J.---George
Statford, 45, was in Fitkin Me-
morial Hospital, this week with
a bullet wound in his right arm
while William Morrison, 72, who
allegedly fired a double-barrel
shotgun at him faced a charge of
assault with intent to kill.
Debutante to Wed
MISS JULIA L. RICHARDSON,
Philadelphia debutante, whose
parents, Mr. and Mrs. Malley
Richardson, announced plans for
her marriage to Earle M. Brown
on December 18, by the Rev.
Sydnor Thomas of St. Barnabas
Church.
The ceremony will be followed
by a reception at the bride's
parents home. Following a short
honeymoon, the newlyweds will
be at home, 1320 N. Park Avenue
after January 1.
