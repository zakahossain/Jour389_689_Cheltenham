Speaker Conlon of the Mary-
land Legislature is fumbling with
the jim-crow repeal bill. That
happens every session. A How-
ard U. law school professor named
Hart personally had the courts
abolish jim crow on inter-State
traffic arteries, but not on trans-
port facilities within the State.
He fought his case through the
lower court to the court of ap-
peals and won.
If we had more lawyers like
the late Professor Hart, jim
crow on all inter-State railroads
would soon end.
As the war lasts two years more
(and I predict it will), jim crow
on trains will be dealt its heaviest
blow by car riders themselves.

Pop Summers
Dr. H. H. (Pop) Summers, Wil-
berforce U. professor of Greek
and theology, who was buried last
week in Harrisburg, Pa., was a
remarkable man. Thirty-six years
ago we attended Howard Uni-
versity together.
Most of us were 18 then;
Pop was 43. He had been a
teacher and principal of a pub-
lic school in Steelton, Pa., for
seventeen years, and with the
late Dr. Numa P. G. Adams,
decided he wanted a college
education.
Being twice as old as the rest
of us didn't bother Pop, neither
did he mind the nickname we
gave him. He was hungry for
knowledge and went on to finish
college, theological school, and
to earn two master's degrees final-
ly at the age of 65.
He thus spent some twenty-
two years in college-teaching
in the winter and studying in
the summer. At the age when
most men are retiring from ac-
tive life, Pop Summers was
just getting ready to do his
best work as a teacher at Wil-
berforce University.
They don't come any more dur-
able and gentlemanly than Pop.
He was a credit to his profession.
