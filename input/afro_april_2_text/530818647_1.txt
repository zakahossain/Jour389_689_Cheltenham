MAYOR TALKS
New Member for Chel-
tenham Advised.
The terms of three mem-
bers of the Board of School
Commissioners expire in
March.
They are, Mrs. John Wesley Brown,
Mrs. Louis H. Levin and John H.
Duncan.
Friends of Dr. B. M. Rhetta, local
physician, who have twice placed his
name before Mayor Broening-once
in his first administration and again
last year when Dr. Frank J. Good-
now, Theodore E. Straus and War-
ren S. Seipp were reappointed-have
indicated already that they expect to
continue the fight to place a colored
representative on the school board.
Other Cities Have Colored
Advocates of this program point
out that under the law, in the Dis-
trict of Columbia, three of the nine
school board members must be col-
ored.
Atlantic City and New York are
other cities which have had colored
school commissioners, and on Janu-
ary first, Mrs. A. H. Martin, a for-
mer Cleveland (Ohio) school teach-
er, was sworn in and took her seat
as the first elected colored member
of the Cleveland school board.
Mayor Gave Promise
Politicians point out that the may-
or in his pre-election speeches made
promise of appointment of colored
on city boards. After election, he
appointed Dr. Howard E. Young to
the Jail Board and Willard Allen up-
on the Zoning Commission. To the
Board of Education, to which his
promise was directed, no appoint-
ment was made.
The Mayor Talks
When asked what was the possi-
bility of a colored member on the
school board this year, Mayor Broen-
ing said:
"While I have not yet fully made
up my mind and am giving the
school board and other matters of
this kind my serious consideration,
I have had so much pressure brought
to bear to retain the old school board
in office that I can give no definite
answer to this question. There would
no doubt be consideration of an ap-
plicant should a vacancy arise, but
there is an agreement to keep the
school board out of politics and not
make class appointments.
"I am being opportuned to keep
the present board in office during
the development of the program they
are putting over. My greatest de-
side is to see that the best is done
for both colored and white children."
Jail Board
Welfare workers have an eye on
the Jail Board, upon which there
has been a vacancy. Parker Emer-
son, white, was appointed by the
mayor, but has failed to qualify.
Effort will be made to have mayor
appoint a colored woman to this
board. They point to the effective
service now being rendered by Mrs.
Grace Hartnett, white, as an indi-
cation of what could be done by a
colored woman member.
Cheltenham
Another appointment which the
mayor will be asked to make this
year is a new city representative up-
on the board of the House of Refor-
mation at Cheltenham. Bailey Con-
way and Joseph P. Evans are the
mayor's appointees on this board at
present.
One of the first moves of the board
several months ago was to oust Dr.
George F. Bragg, who had been a
member of the board for two dec-
ades.
Under the law, Governor Ritchie
is authorized to name annually in
February two members of the Chel-
tenham board. Both of the govern-
ors's anointees are white.
Despite the public clamor against
Cheltenham which has resulted from
"nfavorable Grand Jury reports, and
from refusal of judges to commit
boys there, no changes have been
made by the board in the white per-
sonnel of the reform school.
