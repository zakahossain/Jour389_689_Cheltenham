14 Cheltenham
Inmates Freed
for Holidays
BALTIMORE-
A Christmas present in the form
of freedom was given to four in-
mates of the Cheltenham Reforma-
tory, Saturday, according to Mrs.
Maud Bell, secretary of the Abolish
Cheltenham Committeee, which is
besieging city officials with a bar-
rage of efforts meant to improve
the alleged inhuman treatment of
the youths confined in the institu-
tion.
The hopes of another boy to be
free on Christmas Day were blasted
when officials could not find his
parents, Mrs. Bell said. He was
returned to the institution.
Those released were: John
Thomas Brown, son of a Baptist
minister; Otis Dickerson, Robert
Nettles, and Fitzhugh Briscoe. The
youth whose parents could not be
found was Robert Wiggins.
The father of Brown is the
Rev. Joseph Brown, pastor of the
Prince of Peace Baptist Church.
He has two brothers and four
sisters. The youth, who now
claims he is sixteen years of age,
was sent to the institution three
years ago.
Reports of an investigation last
week of the questionable practice
of hiring the boys out on large
farms at a miserly stipend were
also related by Mrs. Bell in an in-
terview on Thursday.
Boy Paid 50 Cents Weekly
Two boys, William Johnson and
Matthias Ringgold, are now hired
at the Henry Feling estate, Guil-
ford, Howard County, according
to the committee secretary. The
investigator stated that Johnson
claims he only receives fifty cents
a week for his work.
Johnson contends, it was furth-
er stated, that he was fourteen
years of age when he was com-
mitted to the institution in 1920,
but has been working out in ser-
vice since December 23, 1925. Mrs.
Bell said that Johnson claims that
he has been seeking each year,
since he became 21, to secure his
release, but is told that he is not of
age.
Although the youth maintains
that he was born in Baltimore,
no record of his birth was found,
Mrs. Bell stated. His parents,
Mrs. Annie Johnson and Kellum
Johnson, formerly lived on George
Street near Pennsylvania Avenue
but could not be located, it was
further related.
Ringgold is only 13, and accord-
ing to Mrs. Bell, is being denied
educational training despite the
state law requiring youths to at-
tend school until 16.
