Citizens Tell How They're
Paying Their 1942 Taxes
By RUTH JENKINS
BALTIMORE
Despite the fact that the largest
crowds in many years paid income
taxes daily at the Custom House,
inquiries last week revealed that
hundreds of taxpayers were likely
to be caught in the last-minute
rush on the March 15 deadline.
The Custom House at Gay and
Lombard Streets has been kept
open until 9 p.m. to meet the de-
mands of the thousands paying
their taxes in person.
But in order to accommodate
the deadline crowd, it remained
open until midnight on Saturday
and Monday.
Taxes were also paid by check
or by money order, through the
mails.
With the government still con-
sidering phases of the pay-as-you-
go and collection-at-the-souce
systems of taxation, the major-
ity of wage-earners have adopted
the quarterly installment plan for
the payments now due.
Must Meet Deadline
Custom House officials warned
this week that where the quar-
ly payment plan is used, the first
installment or one-fourth of the
tax due must be paid in full by
March 15.
Otherwise, a notice will be sent
the taxpayer, demanding full pay-
ment of the total tax within ten
days, plus a 6 per cent interest
charge.
