ELTON FAX SOON GROUP SENT TO
TO RECOVER
Elton Fax, Baltimore's promising
artist, is still confined in the Johns
Hopkins Hospital after three
months, suffering with a paralytic
condition thought to have been in-
duced by an abscessed tooth.
According to hospital authorities
late Thursday. Mr. Fax was much
improved, although it was not said
when he would be able to leave the
institution. Doctors would not give
the exact nature of Mr. Fax's ail-
ment but assured the AFRO he was
much better.
He has been ill more than three
months, it was said.
Mr. Fax astounded Baltimore this
Spring when he exhibited a collec-
tion of his paintings and sketches in
the AFRO building and was one of
the leading contributors to the art
exhibit of the Women's Co-opera-
tive Civic League, winning its first
award.
He is a graduate of the Syracuse
University School of Art and one of
the most promising of local artists.
N.C. Man Held Here
on Murder
Taken into custody by Detective
Cornelius Roche, John Hines, 27,
1312 Myrtle Avenue, was held for
Elm City, Wilson County, North
Carolina authorities on charges of
murder, Wednesday.
In his statement to local police,
Hines declared that he shot Ernest
Alexander in June, 1930. He lived
in Richmond for a year before being
traced here. He was employed at
the St. Paul Garage.
CHELTENHAM
Four boys, including two home-
less waifs, were ordered sent to the
Cheltenham Reformatory after
hearings in the juvenile court, this
week.
The two orphans, brothers, Win-
field, 15, and Bernard Jones, 17,
were found by a policeman living
in a vacant house. When ques-
tioned by Northern district officers
the boys stated that their parents
were dead and that they had been
living as best they could for five
months. They were sent to the
school to assure a home. The two
boys, police say, were in tatters and
rags when arraigned in the court
and exhibited signs of malnutri-
tion.
Convicted of misdemeanors, Ver-
non Russell, 12, 848 Pierce Street,
and Harry Laroy. 1539 Shield Place,
were ordered sent to the reform
school.
