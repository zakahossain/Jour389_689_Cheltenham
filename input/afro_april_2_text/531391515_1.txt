Ex-Cheltenham Inmate
Gets Twenty-Year Term
BALTIMORE
A 15-year-old former Chelten-
ham inmate, convicted in Crim-
inal Court Thursday of robbing
and criminally assaulting at gun-
point two teen-age girls and at-
tempting to attack another girl,
was sentenced by Judge Row-
land K. Adams to twenty years
in the house of correction.
The youth was actually given a
total of fifty-two years, twenty
years on each of two rape
charges, ten years on the assault
to rape charge and a year each
☐on the two robbery charges, but
☐ Judge Adams ruled that the sen-
tences would run concurrently.
Testimony revealed that the
first attack occurred on January
10, when the boy accosted Miss
Dorothy Beane, 16, of 2521 Wood-
brook Avenue, in the 1800 block
Madison Avenue and forced her
to accompany him to a yard in
the rear of a house in the 1900
block of the same street.
The other attack is said to have
occurred on January 18, when
Miss Dorothy Barney, 18, of 1344
Druid Hill Avenue, was stopped
by the 'accused youth at Eutaw
Place and Lanvale Street and
forced into a nearby yard. With
her at the time was Miss Ruth
Howell, 17, same address, whom
he attempted to attack.
Miss Beane accused the boy of
taking $3.50 from her at the time
of the attack on her and Miss
Howell said he took her watch
and about $10.60. Gregory Haw-
kins represented the boy.
