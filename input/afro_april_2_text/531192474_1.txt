Rev. Douglas
Offered to
Resign Post
BALTIMORE
The Rev. Frederick Douglas
told the Cheltenham Board of
Management and Gov. Harry W.
Nice that he would gladly resign
his post as chief parole officer, it
was learned this week.
In a statement immediately fol-
lowing consideration of Commis-
sioner Jones's report, the Rev.
Mr. Douglas stated, that he ac-
cepted the position because he
was asked to do so, and in the
spirit of being of service to the
institution during its reorganiza-
tion period.
Didn't Seek Appointment
He did not seek the appoint-
ment, he said, and had no idea at
the time that it was in conflict
with any State law.
The Metropolitan pastor,
however, refuted the assertion
that the parole office was
without proper records. He
pointed out that the work of
the office had been highly
praised and that the status of
paroled boys had been great-
ly improved.
Many citizens discussing the
Cheltenham situation, this week,
were inclined to feel that the en-
tire board shared the responsibil-
ity out of which the appointment
of its own members as employees
developed.
Old Guard in Control
Those who have analyzed the
situation declare that the control
of the board is still in the hands
of the old faction which had kept
a strangle-hold on affairs there.
The recently appointed mem-
bers, it was said, have had pro-
gressive ideas, but have been un-
able to put them into execution.
It is known that practically all of
the new members put on some
time before the State took it over,
are favorable to an efficient col-
ored staff.
