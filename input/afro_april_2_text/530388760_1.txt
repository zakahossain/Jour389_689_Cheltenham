JANUARY GRAND JURY
CLOSES ITS WORK.
Visit a Number of Institutions and
Find Them to be in Good
Condition.
The Grand Jury for the January
term of Baltimore City, finished its
work and was discharged with thanks
by Chief Judge Soper, Friday morn-
ing of last week. Judge Soper com-
mended the jury in the highest terms
and spoke of the work accomplished
and the manner in which it was done
as making a record for the members
of the jury.
There were before the jury and act-
ed upon more than nineteen hundred
cases, and in addition to this the jury
visited a large number of penal and
elemosynary institutions. Not the
least of these were the House of the
Good Shepherd for Colored Girls, Fran
klin street and Calverton Road; The
School for Colored Girls at Melvale,
and the House of Reformation for
Colored Boys at Cheltenham. A spec-
ial committee visited the latter in-
stitution and found it to be one of the
best managed in the State. The dor-
mitories, the work shops, class rooms,
chapel,, and in fact every portion of
the institution was visited and every-
the inmates were questioned and as a
rule were as well satisfied as could be
expected under the circumstances.
The play grounds are ample and a
plenty of time is given the boys in
which to exercise themselves. The
school facilities are also up to the stan
dard, and the boy that spends any
time there, can hardly help being
benefitted, as every effort is made to
help him if he will be helped.
The school buildings are models of
comfort and the campu sis one of the
finest in the State. Too much praise
cannot be given the management of
the institution, chief among whom
are Mr. Joseph J. Janey, the secre-
tary: W. W. Sunderland, the general
agent and Manager Pyles. They are
doing a fine work.
