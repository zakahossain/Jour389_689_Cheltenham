53-YEAR-OLD AGENT
NOT WED TO STUDENT
Samuel Frederick Denies Reported
Marriage to Coppin Normal
Senior
A statement that Samuel M.
Frederick of the Frederick Em-
ployment Agency, 1937 Druid Hill
Avenue, had wedded the twenty-
three-year-old Corona Whiting.
Coppin Normal student, was denied
by Mr. Frederick, today.
The employment agent told the
AFRO-AMERICAN that there was
no truth in the report coming to
the office by mail, announcing the
wedding.
Miss Whiting was, until Febru-
ary when she dropped out of the
teacher training school, a senior in
the institution. The report was
also denied by her father, John T.
Whiting.
A letter received by the AFRO
stated that Mr. Frederick, who is
reported as 53 years of age, had
taken as his bride the 23-year-old
student, and that though there was
difference in their ages "she loved
him just the same." The note also
stated that the couple would make
their home at 1937 Druid Hill Ave-
nue.
Trio Ordered Sent
to Reform School
Convicted in the Juvenile Court
on charges of committing one or
more misdemeanors or felonies, 3
boys. were ordered sent to the
Cheltenham Reform School, this
week.
Arraigned in connection with sec-
ond offense misdemeanors the boys
were tried by Judge Thomas Wax-
ter. Following the hearing they
were sent to the school for periods
ranging from one to three years.
The boys who were sent to the
school were George Laprade, 14,
502 Wilson Street: Allen Schaffer.
14. 1112 Parrish Street; and Frank
Cooper, 13, 1358 Cleveland Street.
