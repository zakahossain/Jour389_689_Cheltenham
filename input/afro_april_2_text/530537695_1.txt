Jury Frees Boy Tried
For Cellmate's Death
Upper MarYpro, Aug. 10-Stan-
Jey Jackson, an inmate, of the
House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham, Prince
Georges County, ws acquitted by a
jury of the charge of murdering
William Jones, another inmate, at
the special session of the Circuit
Court here today. During a haz-
ing party staged in the cell Jack-
son was alleged to have struck
Jones over the head with a bucket.
Robert W. Wells, defending
Jackson, contended that the boys
had been confined to the cell for
about twenty days and that the
condition of Jones, owing to lack
of proper food, was the cause of
his death.
