Former Cheltenham Inmate Says
It Is True Guards Club Boys
There.
To the Editor of the AFRO:
I noticed in your issue of this
week's AFRO, September 22, a
picture of boys coming from work
at Cheltenham House of Refor-
mation, and also noted that John
Pyles, white, superintendent, says
it is all right for guards to hit
boys.
In 1925, when I lived in Oak-
land, Md., I wrote your paper
a letter concerning conditions at
the Cheltenham reformatory and
at that time, according to your
paper and the Baltimore Sun, Pyles
was supposed to be fired. It seems
as though he is still in charge.
I am a former inmate of the
institution, and it is true boys are
clubbed. When they are not work-
ing to suit the guards and in the
hottest weather are allowed one-
half of cup of water. They are
cut out of meals hand running three
and four at a time.
This is all for this time, but I
can give you plenty of informa-
tion about Cheltenham.
WILLIAM CREASY
Pennsylvania.
