SCHOOL ROBBER
GANG GOES TO
CHELTENHAM
Say Boys Burglarized
24 Public Schools in
Balto.
SEE INCREASE
IN DELINQUENCY
More Juvenile Arrests
Than in 1932.
Arraigned in the juvenile court
to face charges of participation in
a series of misdemeanors, or being
minors without proper guardian-
ship, nine boys were ordered sent
to the Cheltenham Reform School
by Judge Thomas E. Waxter, this
week.
Two of the boys, John Dixon, 10,
702 Woodyear Street, and Herman
Johnson, 12, 551 Hoffman Street,
were accused of entering 24 public
schools, in every section of the city,
and stealing supplies, money and
other articles from teachers' desks,
and of tearing down pay telephones
in the schools and robbing the
boxes.
Increase Noted
The other boys who were impli-
cated in violations were given terms
at the school ranging from one to
two years. Statistics of the court
show that during the first quarter
the present year there has been an
increase of 21 juvenile arrests in
comparison with the same period
of 1932.
Those sent to the school were:
James Hammond, 13, 863 Vine;
Samuel Murray, 13, 1425 Brunt;
John Perry, 17, Alexandria, Va.;
James Floyd, 1105 Madison; Wm.
Stokes, 16, 300 W. 20th; John Dix,
702 Woodyear; Herman Johnson,
12, 551 Hoffman; Harry Brown, 14,
633 Central; Raymond Homer, 15,
1002 Carey.
