Drop Charges
Against Clarke
af Cheltenham
White Superintendent
Lifts Suspension of
Educational Director
BALTIMORE
Charges of insubordination
against Daniel Clarke, education-
al supervisor at Cheltenham
School for Boys, have been with-
drawn and his suspension lifted,
it was announced Thursday.
Col. Harry C. Jones, head of
the State Employment Commis-
sion which must pass on suspen-
sions and dismissals at State in-
stitutions, told the AFRO that
his office had received notice of
withdrawal of the charges by
Vance E. Thomas, white, superin-
tendent of the school.
Howard C. Hill, white, acting
president of the school's board of
managers, said that Mr. Clarke
returned to work Monday and
that he and Mr. Thomas had
reached a working agreement.
Suspended on May 17
Mr. Clarke, the only colored ad-
ministrative employee at the in-
stitution, was suspended May 17
by Mr. Thomas for refusing to
teach a class.
However, those close to the
Cheltenham situation saw in the
suspension an attempt by the ad-
ministration to punish Mr. Clarke
for joining with other employees
who have insisted upon an end
to segregation of colored and
white staff members at the school.
It is also known that Mr. Clarke
and other employees have insist-
ed that the administration should
carry out the order of the board
of managers issued March 25,
ending segregation in the cafe-
teria and housing facilities and a
subsequent order urging replace-
ment of white employees with
colored.
Board in 7-Hour Session
The board held a seven-hour
meeting last Tuesday at which
time some of the difficulties
were ironed out, but no statement
was made. It is expected that
the board will hold another meet-
ing this week.
Eight colored employees have
resigned from the school staff in
the past two months on account
of what they term "bungling" of
the question of equal facilities for
staff members by Mr. Thomas.
