Calls Cheltenham
A Real Hell
Cheltenham is a real Hell Attor-
ney Josiah Henry told the AFRO-
AMERICAN this week following a
hearing in which he failed by ha-
beous corpus to secure the release
of Paul Cook, age 20, from the State
reformatory.
Cook was brought to Baltimore
from Cheltenham by order of his
sister who lives in Philadelphia.
She said her brother was commit.
ted at the age of 15 as a minor
without proper care, and that she
had provided a home for him now
and wanted him with her.
The boy who is hired out to a
white family and is under the charge
of a guide while in the city ap-
peared frightened and told the court
he didn't want to leave.
:
"Cook." said Attorney Henry, "is
hired out under what is really a
slave contract. He wanted to come
but was afraid to say, so for fear
there might be some slip and the
guard would take him back and
beat him up.
"The real menace of Cheltenham
is that it takes away the manhood
of every boy incarcerated there."
