Suspicions of
Teacher Aid in
Arrest of Seven

BALTIMORE
During most of last week, four
young boys lavishly treated
themselves; their chums, and
jingled coins in their pockets
while in school until a suspicious
teacher reported the matter to
Policewoman Violet H. Whyte
who solved the mystery of a
missing $90.

As a result, four boys, all un-
der 15 years of age, were given
a hearing on Tuesday in Ju-
venile Court. The ringleader was
committed to Cheltenham, one of
the boys was held for further
investigation, while the other two
were placed on probation.

3 Face Jury
Three persons, Mrs. Mary
Richards, 50, her son, Charles, 18,
of 1843 N. Gilmor Street, and
Nathaniel E. Toyer, 19, of 1026
Lexington Street, who were
charged with receiving some of
the stolen money, were placed
under $250 bail each by Mag-
istrate J. H. Stanford for grand
jury action.

On Monday, January 13, James
Sandy, white, of 1601 Westmin-
ster Avenue, told police that while
eating in a store near Fulton Av-
enue and Baker Street, someone
had stolen $90 from a compart-
ment in his truck which had been
parked nearby. The money rep-
resented collections for the
bakery for which he worked.

Sandy said he did not discover
the loss until after he had re-
ported at the bakery office.
Returning to the scene, he
searched for several small boys
he had observed loitering about,
but failed to find them.

Teacher Gives Clue
A few days later, Policewom-
an Whyte was told by a school-
teacher of the unusual amount
of money several of her pupils
had in their possession. Her
subsequent investigation led to
the roundup of the boys and the
persons charged with receiving
the money. Sergeant Thomas
Joyce aided in the roundup.

In signed statements, the boys
admitted stealing the money. They
said they gave Mrs. Richards, $1
for allowing them to divide the
cash on her kitchen table, and
gave some money to her son and
young daughter and to Toyer.

Sandy was unable to identify
the boys at the hearing. He said
they appeared to be of the same
size as the ones he had observed
when he parked his truck.
